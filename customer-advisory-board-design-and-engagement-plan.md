## Executive Summary

A Customer Advisory Board (CAB) is a strategic group of high-value customers who provide ongoing feedback, insights, and guidance on product direction, strategy, and market fit. For ConectaPay, the CAB will serve as a critical feedback loop to accelerate product-market fit validation, reduce churn, generate case studies, and identify expansion revenue opportunities.

### Business Case

**Investment**: R$ 15.000-25.000/year (12 members @ R$ 1.250-2.000/year in incentives)
**Expected Return**: R$ 150.000-300.000/year (10-20x ROI)
- **Churn Reduction**: 3-5% absolute reduction from engaged advocates (~R$ 90K/year saved)
- **Expansion Revenue**: 15-25% increase in expansion from CAB members (~R$ 120K/year)
- **Referrals**: 2-3 referrals per CAB member (~30-40 new customers, ~R$ 60K/year in ACV)
- **Product Insights**: Faster PMF validation, reduced development waste (~R$ 30K/year saved)
- **Case Studies**: 12 high-quality case studies/year for marketing (~R$ 50K value in agency fees)

### Key Objectives

1. **Product Validation**: Test features, messaging, and pricing with real customers before market launch
2. **Churn Reduction**: Build deep relationships with high-value customers, reducing churn by 30-50%
3. **Expansion Revenue**: Identify and close expansion opportunities 2x faster
4. **Advocacy**: Generate referrals, case studies, and testimonials at scale
5. **Market Intelligence**: Stay ahead of market trends, competitive threats, and customer needs

### Target Launch

**CAB Formation**: Month 3-4 (after 50 paying customers)
**First Meeting**: Month 4
**Cadence**: Quarterly (every 3 months)
**Initial Size**: 12 members (3 per primary segment)

## Part 1: CAB Structure and Governance

### 1.1 CAB Model: Two-Tier Design

ConectaPay will use a **two-tier CAB model** to balance depth of engagement with scalability:

#### Tier 1: Strategic CAB (12 members)
- **Frequency**: Quarterly meetings (90 minutes, virtual)
- **Membership**: 3 members per segment (Clínicas, Imobiliárias, Academias, Óticas)
- **Investment**: R$ 1.500-2.500/year per member in incentives
- **Access**: Direct access to CEO, Product, Customer Success
- **Commitment**: 1-year term, renewable

#### Tier 2: CAB Ambassadors (30-40 members)
- **Frequency**: Monthly pulse surveys, annual 1:1 interviews
- **Membership**: Top 10% of customers by CLV and engagement
- **Investment**: R$ 200-500/year per member in incentives
- **Access**: Monthly newsletter, exclusive beta access
- **Commitment**: Ongoing, based on performance

### 1.2 Governance Structure

**CAB Executive Sponsor**: CEO or CMO (owns relationship, attends meetings)
**CAB Program Manager**: Customer Success Lead (coordinates logistics, meeting prep)
**CAB Liaison**: Product Manager (synthesizes feedback, shares roadmap updates)

**Quarterly CAB Meeting Agenda**:
1. Welcome & Business Update (10 min) - CEO/CMO
2. Product Roadmap Review (20 min) - Product
3. Feature Feedback & Co-Creation (30 min) - Interactive session
4. Market Trends & Competitive Insights (15 min) - Open discussion
5. Success Story Spotlight (10 min) - CAB member shares use case
6. Action Items & Next Steps (5 min) - Program Manager

### 1.3 CAB Member Roles

**Primary Responsibilities**:
- Attend quarterly meetings (90 minutes, virtual)
- Provide honest, constructive feedback on product and strategy
- Test beta features and provide detailed feedback
- Participate in 2-3 1:1 interviews per year
- Serve as references for case studies and referrals (when appropriate)

**Time Commitment**: 8-10 hours/year (4 meetings + prep + ad-hoc feedback)

**Compensation**: R$ 1.500-2.500/year in:
- Service credits (50%): ConectaPay service credits
- Amazon gift cards (30%): Direct compensation
- Exclusive perks (20%): Early access, VIP support, co-marketing

## Part 2: CAB Member Selection Criteria

### 2.1 Ideal Customer Profile for CAB

**MUST-HAVE Criteria (Required)**:
- [x] **Active Customer**: 3+ months as paying customer
- [x] **High CLV**: Platinum or Gold tier (R$ 5K+ lifetime value or projected)
- [x] **Product Engagement**: Active usage (70%+ feature adoption, no missed payments)
- [x] **Advocacy Potential**: NPS 9-10, has referred or willing to refer
- [x] **Strategic Fit**: Aligns with target ICP (segment, size, tech maturity)

**NICE-TO-HAVE Criteria (Bonus points)**:
- [ ] **Multi-Location**: 2+ locations or franchises
- [ ] **Decision-Maker**: Owner, CEO, or Director level
- [ ] **Industry Influence**: Active in industry associations, speaks at events
- [ ] **Tech-Savvy**: Uses other SaaS products, comfortable with beta testing
- [ ] **Geographic Diversity**: Different cities/regions (not all in São Paulo)

### 2.3 Segment-Specific Criteria

#### Clínicas Médicas (3 members)
- **Ideal Profile**: Multi-specialty clinic, 5-20 doctors, R$ 50K-500K/month revenue
- **Pain Point**: High no-show rate (15%+), actively trying to reduce
- **Tech Maturity**: Uses electronic health records (EHR), practice management SaaS
- **Decision-Maker**: Clinic owner or operations manager

#### Imobiliárias (3 members)
- **Ideal Profile**: 10-50 agents, 20-100 active listings, R$ 30K-300K/month revenue
- **Pain Point**: Lead conversion issues (20%+ lost leads), manual follow-up
- **Tech Maturity**: Uses real estate CRM (Loft, ZapImóveis), WhatsApp Business
- **Decision-Maker**: Brokerage owner or sales director

#### Academias (3 members)
- **Ideal Profile**: 500-2.000 members, R$ 20K-150K/month revenue
- **Pain Point**: High churn (8%+ monthly), manual payment collection
- **Tech Maturity**: Uses gym management SaaS (TotalPass, Minotá), WhatsApp
- **Decision-Maker**: Gym owner or operations manager

#### Óticas (3 members)
- **Ideal Profile**: 2-5 stores, R$ 15K-100K/month revenue
- **Pain Point**: Manual appointment reminders, payment recovery
- **Tech Maturity**: Uses POS system, WhatsApp Business
- **Decision-Maker**: Store owner or manager

**Customer Tenure Balance**:

## Part 3: CAB Recruitment Strategy

### 3.1 Recruitment Timeline

**Phase 1: Identification (Week 1-2)**
- Export customer list from CRM
- Score all customers using CAB qualification scorecard
- Identify top 25-30 candidates (allow for 50% decline rate)

**Phase 2: Outreach (Week 3-4)**
- Personalized invitations from CEO/CMO
- 1:1 calls to explain program, answer questions
- Collect availability and confirm interest

**Phase 3: Selection (Week 5)**
- Final selection of 12 members (3 per segment)
- Waitlist of 8-10 backups
- Send acceptance packets

**Phase 4: Onboarding (Week 6)**
- Welcome package (digital + physical)
- Pre-meeting survey (topics of interest, availability)
- Schedule first quarterly meeting

**Phase 5: First Meeting (Week 8)**
- Kickoff meeting (90 minutes)
- Set expectations, build rapport
- Establish meeting cadence

### 3.3 Outreach Sequence (4 touches)

**Touch 1: Email from CEO** (above)
- **Wait**: 3 business days

**Touch 2: Call from CSM** (if no response)
- **Script**: "Oi [Nome], vi que você recebeu o convite para o CAB. Queria saber se você tem alguma dúvida? São só 15 minutos."

**Touch 3: WhatsApp from CEO** (if no response)
- **Script**: "Oi [Nome]! Tudo bem? Gostaria de convidar você para o Conselho Consultivo da ConectaPay. É um grupo seleto de nossos melhores clientes. Posso te ligar 5 min para explicar?"

### 3.4 Managing Declines and Waitlist

**If They Decline**:
- Thank them warmly: "Entendo perfeitamente. Obrigado por considerar!"
- Ask if they want to be part of **Tier 2 Ambassadors** (lighter commitment)
- Keep them in the loop: "Ainda vamos compartilhar atualizações com você"
- Re-invite in 12 months if circumstances change

**If They Accept**:
- Send welcome packet within 24 hours
- Add to CAB Slack/Discord channel
- Schedule 1:1 onboarding call (30 min)
- Collect meeting availability via Calendly

**Waitlist Management**:
- If a CAB member churns or steps down, invite from waitlist within 2 weeks
- Rotate waitlist members into CAB every 12 months for fresh perspectives

## Part 4: CAB Engagement Plan

### 4.1 Meeting Cadence and Format

**Quarterly CAB Meeting** (Tier 1, 12 members)
- **Duration**: 90 minutes
- **Format**: Virtual (Google Meet/Zoom)
- **Frequency**: Every 3 months (March, June, September, December)
- **Time**: 10:00-11:30 AM BRT (Tuesday or Wednesday)
- **Attendance Goal**: 80%+ (9-10 members)

**Meeting Structure**:
```
0:00-0:10  Welcome & Business Update (CEO/CMO)
0:10-0:30  Product Roadmap Review (Product)
0:30-1:00  Feature Feedback & Co-Creation (Interactive)
1:00-1:15  Market Trends & Competitive Insights (Discussion)
1:15-1:25  Success Story Spotlight (CAB Member)
1:25-1:30  Action Items & Next Steps
```

**Annual CAB Summit** (Tier 1 + Tier 2, 40-50 members)
- **Duration**: Half-day (4 hours)
- **Format**: Virtual or in-person (São Paulo)
- **Frequency**: Once per year (February/March)
- **Purpose**: Year-in-review, roadmap preview, networking, awards

### 4.2 Pre-Meeting Preparation (2 weeks before)

**Week 2 Before**:
- [ ] Send meeting invite with calendar hold
- [ ] Share pre-meeting survey (topics to prioritize, availability)
- [ ] Collect product feedback from last quarter

**Week 1 Before**:
- [ ] Share meeting agenda and materials (roadmap, mockups)
- [ ] Send prep questions (1-2 discussion topics)
- [ ] Confirm attendance (remind 48 hours before)

**Day Before**:
- [ ] Final attendance confirmation
- [ ] Test tech setup (Zoom, Miro/FigJam for collaboration)
- [ ] Prepare facilitation guide and timekeeper

### 4.3 Post-Meeting Follow-Up (Within 48 hours)

**Within 24 Hours**:
- [ ] Send meeting notes and action items
- [ ] Share recording (if approved)
- [ ] Thank participants via email/Slack

**Within 48 Hours**:
- [ ] Update product backlog with CAB feedback
- [ ] Assign action items to owners (Product, CS, Engineering)
- [ ] Share CAB insights with broader company (all-hands, Slack)

**Within 1 Week**:
- [ ] Follow up on promised items (e.g., "send me that case study")
- [ ] Schedule 1:1 calls for members who wanted more discussion
- [ ] Update CAB portal (roadmap changes, upcoming features)

### 4.4 Ongoing Engagement (Between Meetings)

**Monthly Pulse Survey** (5 minutes):
- Product satisfaction (NPS, CSAT)
- Feature requests and prioritization
- Competitive intelligence (what are you seeing?)
- Health check (any issues?)

**Monthly CAB Newsletter**:
- Product roadmap updates
- New feature announcements
- CAB member spotlight (success story)
- Upcoming events and webinars

**Ad-Hoc Engagement**:
- Slack/Discord channel for real-time feedback
- Beta testing invitations (new features)
- Case study interviews (2-3x/year)
- Referral requests (when appropriate)

**Annual 1:1 Interviews** (30-60 minutes):
- Deep dive on customer journey
- Expansion opportunity assessment
- Churn risk assessment
- Strategic feedback on ConectaPay direction

### 4.5 CAB Success Stories and Recognition

**CAB Member Spotlight** (monthly):
- Feature one CAB member in newsletter and social media
- Highlight their success, use cases, and ROI
- Tag them on LinkedIn/Instagram (with permission)

**CAB Awards** (annual):
- **Innovator of the Year**: Most creative use of ConectaPay

## Part 7: CAB Feedback Loop and Product Integration

### 7.1 CAB Feedback Collection Methods

**Real-Time Feedback**:
- Slack/Discord channel for ad-hoc questions and ideas
- In-meeting feedback (live during quarterly meetings)
- Beta testing surveys (after testing new features)

**Structured Feedback**:
- Monthly pulse survey (5 minutes, 5-10 questions)
- Quarterly deep-dive survey (15 minutes, pre-meeting)
- Annual 1:1 interview (30-60 minutes, strategic feedback)

**Reactive Feedback**:
- Customer support tickets (tagged as CAB member)
- Churn risk surveys (if engagement drops)
- Exit interviews (if CAB member leaves)

### 7.2 Feedback Synthesis and Prioritization

**Weekly Feedback Review** (Product Manager + CS Lead):
- Review all CAB feedback from past week
- Categorize by type: bug, feature request, strategic insight, competitive intel
- Tag by segment: Clínicas, Imobiliárias, Academias, Óticas
- Identify patterns: 3+ members mentioning same issue = high priority

**Monthly CAB Report** (to Product and Engineering):
- Top 5 feature requests (with vote count)
- Top 3 bugs or pain points
- Competitive intelligence summary
- Customer quotes and testimonials
- Recommended roadmap changes

**Quarterly Roadmap Review** (with CAB input):
- Present proposed roadmap to CAB for validation
- Collect feedback and prioritization votes
- Adjust roadmap based on CAB input (30-40% adoption rate target)
- Communicate changes back to CAB (closed loop)

### 7.3 Closed-Loop Communication

**Feedback Acknowledgement**:
- Within 48 hours: "Thank you for the feedback on [feature]. We've added it to our backlog."
- Within 2 weeks: "We're prioritizing this for [quarter]. Here's the timeline."
- Upon completion: "Your feedback helped shape [feature]. Here's what we built."

**Transparency on Rejection**:
- If feedback is rejected: "We heard your request for [feature], but we're not prioritizing it because [reason]. Here's what we're focusing on instead."
- Always explain the "why" to maintain trust

**Public Recognition**:
- When a CAB idea makes it to production: "This feature was inspired by feedback from [CAB Member Name]. Thank you!"
- Feature release notes include "Suggested by our CAB members"

## Part 8: CAB Risk Management and Mitigation

### 8.1 Common Risks and Mitigation Strategies

**Risk 1: Low Engagement / Attendance**
- **Symptoms**: <50% meeting attendance, <50% survey response
- **Root Causes**: Meeting fatigue, unclear value, time constraints
- **Mitigation**:
  - Reduce meeting frequency (quarterly → semi-annual)
  - Shorten meetings (90 → 60 minutes)
  - Increase compensation or perks
  - 1:1 outreach to understand disengagement
  - Replace underperforming members

**Risk 2: Negative Sentiment / Bad Apple**
- **Symptoms**: CAB member consistently negative, toxic in meetings
- **Root Causes**: Unresolved product issues, personality mismatch, unrealistic expectations
- **Mitigation**:
  - 1:1 call to understand concerns
  - Resolve legitimate product issues quickly
  - If unresolvable: politely remove from CAB (with 30-day notice)
  - Replace with waitlist member

**Risk 3: Competitor Poaching**
- **Symptoms**: CAB member joins competitor, shares ConectaPay insights
- **Root Causes**: Competitor offers better features/pricing, strategic move
- **Mitigation**:
  - NDA or CAB agreement (confidentiality clause)
  - Focus on relationship value (not just incentives)
  - Monitor for sudden disengagement or competitive usage
  - Exit process: revoke CAB access, thank them for contributions

**Risk 4: Lack of Diversity / Groupthink**
- **Symptoms**: All CAB members agree on everything, no dissenting opinions
- **Root Causes**: Homogeneous selection, fear of speaking up
- **Mitigation**:
  - Intentionally recruit diverse perspectives (segments, company sizes, geographies)
  - Anonymous feedback option (surveys)
  - Facilitated discussions (ensure quiet voices are heard)
  - "Devil's advocate" role (rotate among members)

**Risk 5: Over-Commitment to CAB Requests**
- **Symptoms**: Product roadmap dominated by CAB requests, ignores market needs
- **Root Causes**: CAB bias, lack of market validation
- **Mitigation**:
  - CAB input = 30-40% of roadmap, not 100%
  - Validate CAB requests with broader customer base
  - Product team maintains final decision authority
  - Segment CAB vs. non-CAB feedback

### 8.2 CAB Member Offboarding Process

**Voluntary Exit** (member steps down):
- Thank them warmly for contributions
- Conduct exit interview (15-30 minutes)
- Revoke CAB access (Slack, portal, beta access)
- Invite to Tier 2 Ambassadors (if interested)
- Replace from waitlist within 2 weeks

**Involuntary Exit** (ConectaPay removes member):
- 30-day notice period
- Clear explanation (e.g., low engagement, policy violation)
- Graceful transition (maintain customer relationship)
- Replace from waitlist

**Churn Exit** (member cancels ConectaPay):
- Standard churn process
- CAB-specific exit interview (what did we miss?)
- Attempt to save (offer retention incentive)
- If lost: learn from churn, prevent similar issues

## Appendix A: CAB Member Agreement (Optional)

**Note**: This is a soft agreement (not legally binding) to set expectations. Can be formalized into an NDA if needed.

**Conselho Consultivo de Clientes (CAB) - Termos de Participação**

**1. Propósito**
O CAB da ConectaPay tem como objetivo reunir clientes valiosos para fornecer feedback sobre produtos, estratégias e direção do mercado.

**2. Compromissos da ConectaPay**
- Realizar 4 reuniões virtuais por ano (90 minutos cada)
- Coletar e agir sobre o feedback dos membros
- Fornecer benefícios exclusivos (acesso antecipado, suporte VIP, co-marketing)
- Compensação: R$ 1.500-2.500/year em créditos de serviço, gift cards e benefícios
- Respeitar a confidencialidade das informações compartilhadas

**3. Compromissos do Membro**
- Participar de 4 reuniões virtuais por ano (90 minutos cada)
- Fornecer feedback honesto e construtivo
- Testar recursos beta e compartilhar opiniões
- Participar de 2-3 entrevistas 1:1 por ano (30-60 minutos cada)
- Servir como referência para cases e indicações (quando apropriado)
- Tempo estimado: 8-10 horas/ano

**4. Confidencialidade**
- Informações compartilhadas nas reuniões do CAB são confidenciais
- Roadmaps e recursos não anunciados não devem ser compartilhados externamente
- A ConectaPay pode compartilhar o nome e logo do membro como parte do CAB (com permissão)

**5. Termo e Renovação**
- Compromisso inicial de 1 ano
- Renovação automática a menos que uma das partes decida encerrar
- 30 dias de aviso prévio para encerramento por qualquer parte

**6. Encerramento**
- Qualquer parte pode encerrar a participação a qualquer momento
- Membros que saem perdem acesso a benefícios exclusivos do CAB
- A ConectaPay mantém o direito de usar feedback já fornecido
