## Executive Summary

### Mission
Proactively drive customer adoption, value realization, and long-term retention through structured customer engagement at every lifecycle stage.

### Vision
Become the trusted partner for Brazilian SMBs, turning every customer into a success story and advocate.

### Alignment with Company Goals
- **Revenue Goal**: Achieve 110% Net Revenue Retention (NRR) by Year 1
- **Retention Goal**: Reduce churn to <5% monthly by Q4
- **Expansion Goal**: 30% of revenue from upsells/cross-sells by Month 12
- **Advocacy Goal**: 40% customer referral rate by Month 12

### CS Philosophy
**"Proactive over Reactive, Value over Activity, Outcomes over Outputs"**

## 3. Operating Model and Cadences

### Daily Rhythm

**07:00 - 08:00 | Health Score Monitoring**
- CS Ops: Run daily health score calculations
- Flag accounts dropping below 70 (yellow) and 50 (red)
- Send alerts to assigned CSMs

**08:00 - 09:00 | Triage and Planning**
- CSMs: Review health alerts, prioritize at-risk accounts
- Plan daily customer touchpoints
- Identify urgent escalations

**09:00 - 12:00 | Customer Touchpoints (Block 1)**
- CSMs: Customer calls, meetings, check-ins
- Onboarding: New customer kickoffs
- Advocacy: Advocate outreach

**12:00 - 13:00 | Lunch**

**13:00 - 15:00 | Customer Touchpoints (Block 2)**
- CSMs: Continue customer meetings
- Office hours (Growth CSM)
- Training sessions (Onboarding)

**15:00 - 16:00 | Admin and Follow-up**
- CSMs: CRM updates, call notes, action items
- Email/WhatsApp follow-ups
- Prepare for next day's meetings

**16:00 - 17:00 | Collaboration and Learning**
- Cross-functional syncs (Product, Sales)
- Team training (CS Ops - Training)
- Playbook development
- Account planning

**17:00 - 17:30 | End-of-Day Review**
- Review health score changes
- Log key activities and insights
- Flag blockers for next day

---

## 4. Customer Lifecycle Management

### Lifecycle Stages and CS Ownership

**Onboarding (Days 0-14)** | Onboarding Specialist
- **Goal**: Time-to-value <7 days
- **Activities**: Kickoff call, account setup, integration, training, first win
- **Metrics**: Onboarding completion rate, TTV, activation rate
- **Handoff**: To CSM when activation score ≥80%

**Adoption (Days 15-60)** | CSM (All Tiers)
- **Goal**: Feature adoption ≥80% of core features
- **Activities**: Weekly/bi-weekly check-ins, feature training, best practices
- **Metrics**: Feature adoption rate, usage frequency, engagement score
- **Trigger**: Expansion nurture when adoption ≥90%

**Growth (Days 61-180)** | CSM (All Tiers)
- **Goal**: Value realization and expansion
- **Activities**: Monthly check-ins, value milestones, expansion opportunities
- **Metrics**: Expansion revenue, upgrade rate, NRR
- **Trigger**: Advocacy nurture when NPS ≥50

**Maturity (Days 181+)** | CSM (Enterprise/Scale) + Advocacy Lead
- **Goal**: Retention and advocacy
- **Activities**: Quarterly EBRs/QBRs, advisory board, case studies, referrals
- **Metrics**: Retention rate, NPS, referral rate, case study participation
- **Trigger**: Renewal nurture at Day 330

**Renewal (Day 330-365)** | CSM + Sales
- **Goal**: 95%+ renewal rate
- **Activities**: Renewal outreach, value recap, objection handling, contract negotiation
- **Metrics**: Renewal rate, renewal timeline, discount rate
- **Handoff**: To Sales for contract negotiation (Enterprise/Scale)

**At-Risk (Any Stage)** | CSM + CS Ops
- **Goal**: Churn prevention and recovery
- **Activities**: Risk assessment, playbook execution, executive escalation, win-back campaigns
- **Metrics**: Churn rate, recovery rate, save rate
- **Trigger**: Health score drops below 70 (yellow) or 50 (red)

---

### Lifecycle Transition Triggers

**Onboarding → Adoption**
- Activation score ≥80% (3+ core features used, 5+ WhatsApp messages sent, 1+ Pix payment received)
- Customer completes onboarding checklist
- CSM sign-off on readiness

**Adoption → Growth**
- 30+ days active usage
- 80%+ feature adoption
- Customer expresses satisfaction with value
- No outstanding blockers

**Growth → Maturity**
- 180+ days as customer
- Consistent usage (no drop-offs >7 days)
- Expansion or upsell accepted
- NPS score ≥40

**Maturity → Renewal**
- Day 330 trigger (30 days before renewal)
- Renewal health score ≥80
- No outstanding issues or escalations
- Positive value realization confirmed

**Any Stage → At-Risk**
- Health score drops below 70 (yellow) or 50 (red)
- Usage decline >30% for 7+ days
- Payment failure or late payment
- Support sentiment negative (2+ tickets with negative sentiment)
- Executive escalation or competitor mention

## 7. Cross-Functional Integration

### CS ↔ Product

**Weekly Sync (Thursdays 14:00-15:00)**
- **Attendees**: Head of CS, CSM leads, Product Manager, Engineering Lead
- **Agenda**:
  - Top 5 customer feature requests
  - Bug reports and prioritization
  - Onboarding friction points
  - Upcoming feature releases and CS enablement

**Feedback Loop**
- CSMs log feature requests in central tracker (Google Form → Sheets)
- Product reviews weekly, prioritizes based on:
  - Number of requests (weighted by segment)
  - Revenue impact (Enterprise > Scale > Growth > Start)
  - Strategic alignment
- Product communicates roadmap to CS monthly
- CS enables team 2 weeks before feature launch

**Shared KPIs**
- Feature adoption rate (Product builds, CS drives)
- Time-to-resolution for bugs (Product fixes, CS communicates)
- Customer satisfaction with features (joint ownership)

---

### CS ↔ Sales

**Weekly Sync (Mondays 15:00-16:00)**
- **Attendees**: Head of CS, CSM leads, Sales Lead, Account Executives
- **Agenda**:
  - New customer handoffs (Sales → CS)
  - Renewal pipeline (CS → Sales)
  - Expansion opportunities (CS identifies, Sales closes)
  - At-risk accounts and churn prevention (joint)

**Handoff Process (Sales → CS)**
- **Timing**: Within 24 hours of deal closed
- **Handoff Document**: Customer profile, use case, expectations, stakeholders
- **Kickoff Call**: Sales introduces CS to customer (optional for Enterprise/Scale)
- **SLA**: Sales responds to CS renewal/expansion inquiries within 24h

**Renewal Process (CS → Sales)**
- **Timing**: 45 days before renewal (Day 320)
- **Renewal Packet**: Value recap, usage data, expansion opportunities
- **Sales Action**: Reach out, negotiate renewal, close expansion
- **SLA**: CS provides complete packet, Sales closes renewal

**Shared KPIs**
- Revenue retention (CS owns health, Sales owns renewal)
- Expansion revenue (CS identifies, Sales closes)
- Customer satisfaction (joint ownership)

---

### CS ↔ Marketing

**Weekly Sync (Mondays 14:00-15:00)**
- **Attendees**: Head of CS, Advocacy Lead, Marketing Lead, Content Lead
- **Agenda**:
  - Case study pipeline (Advocacy produces, Marketing distributes)
  - Customer testimonials and quotes
  - Referral program performance
  - Customer feedback for messaging

**Content Collaboration**
- Advocacy produces 4-6 case studies/month (CON-164)
- Marketing distributes across channels (web, social, email, sales)
- CS provides customer access and approval coordination
- Marketing tracks case study performance (leads, conversions)

**Referral Program**
- Marketing designs referral program (CON-97)
- CS executes and manages customer participation
- Marketing creates promotional assets and campaigns
- Joint tracking of referrals and conversion

**Shared KPIs**
- Case study production rate (Advocacy produces, Marketing supports)
- Referral rate (CS drives, Marketing enables)
- Customer advocacy (NPS, testimonials, reviews)

---

### CS ↔ Support

**Daily Sync (Asynchronous via Slack/WhatsApp)**
- **Attendees**: CSMs, Support Lead, Support Agents
- **Agenda**: Escalations, trends, product issues

**Escalation Process**
- **Level 1**: Support handles routine inquiries (FAQ, how-to, bugs)
- **Level 2**: Support escalates to CSM for at-risk customers, strategic accounts
- **Level 3**: CSM escalates to Product for critical bugs, feature requests
- **SLA**: Support responds within 4h, CSM responds within 24h

**Shared KPIs**
- Customer satisfaction (CSAT)
- First response time (Support owns, CS monitors)
- Resolution time (Support owns, CS escalates if stuck)

### CS ROI Calculation

**Year 1 ROI**
- **Investment**: R$ 905,100
- **Assumptions**:
  - 500 paying customers by end of Year 1
  - Average ARPU: R$ 500/month
  - Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR): R$ 3M
  - CS attributable revenue retention: 95% (vs 80% without CS)
  - CS attributable expansion: 20% of revenue

  - Retention impact: R$ 3M × (95% - 80%) = R$ 450,000 saved

## 11. Risk Assessment and Mitigation

### Key Risks

**Risk 1: Hiring Delays**
- **Impact**: High - Cannot scale customer engagement
- **Probability**: Medium - Brazil market competitive for CS talent
- **Mitigation**:
  - Start hiring early (Month 1)
  - Use multiple channels (LinkedIn, referrals, local job boards)
  - Offer competitive salaries and benefits
  - Consider remote candidates outside São Paulo
  - Have backup candidates

**Risk 2: Low Health Score Accuracy**
- **Impact**: High - Miss at-risk customers, wasted effort
- **Probability**: Medium - Health scoring is complex
- **Mitigation**:
  - Start with simple model (usage + engagement)
  - Iterate weekly based on feedback
  - Validate health scores with CSMs
  - Add more factors over time
  - Have manual override process

**Risk 3: Poor Cross-Functional Alignment**
- **Impact**: High - Siloed work, poor customer experience
- **Probability**: Medium - New function, unclear boundaries
- **Mitigation**:
  - Establish weekly syncs from Day 1
  - Document clear handoff processes
  - Define shared KPIs
  - Involve stakeholders in CS planning
  - Escalate misalignments quickly

**Risk 4: High Churn Before CS Fully Operational**
- **Impact**: High - Lost revenue, negative word-of-mouth
- **Probability**: High - Early-stage company, product gaps
- **Mitigation**:
  - Prioritize onboarding (first touchpoint)
  - Launch at-risk playbooks early
  - Provide proactive support to high-value customers
  - Gather and act on customer feedback quickly
  - Set realistic expectations with customers

**Risk 5: Low ROI / High CS Cost**
- **Impact**: Medium - Executive scrutiny, budget cuts
- **Probability**: Medium - CS is expensive early on
- **Mitigation**:
  - Start with lean team (3 FTEs in Month 1)
  - Focus on high-value segments first (Enterprise, Scale)
  - Automate repetitive tasks (health scoring, emails)
  - Track and communicate ROI early and often
  - Scale team as revenue scales

**Risk 6: Low Adoption of CS Processes**
- **Impact**: Medium - Inconsistent customer experience
- **Probability**: Medium - New processes, change management
- **Mitigation**:
  - Involve team in process design
  - Train and enable team thoroughly
  - Lead by example (leadership uses processes)
  - Gather feedback and iterate
  - Recognize and reward adherence

**Risk 7: Customer Over-Engagement (Annoyance)**
- **Impact**: Medium - Negative customer sentiment
- **Probability**: Medium - Over-enthusiastic CSMs
- **Mitigation**:
  - Define touch frequency by segment (not one-size-fits-all)
  - Train CSMs on reading customer signals
  - Monitor engagement metrics (response rates, meeting attendance)
  - Gather feedback on engagement frequency
  - Empower customers to opt-out or adjust frequency

**Risk 8: Tech Stack Complexity / Failure**
- **Impact**: Medium - Disrupted operations, poor data
- **Probability**: Low - Choosing proven tools
- **Mitigation**:
  - Start with simple, proven tools
  - Have backup processes (manual workarounds)
  - Train team on tool troubleshooting
  - Monitor tool performance and uptime
  - Have vendor support contacts

### Appendix B: Playbook Template

**Playbook: [Name]**

**Trigger**: Health score drops below 70 (yellow) or 50 (red)

**Goal**: Recover at-risk customer and prevent churn

**Owner**: CSM (assigned by segment)

**Timeline**: Execute within 24 hours of trigger

**Steps**:

1. **Assess Risk (30 min)**
   - Review health score history and trends
   - Identify drop-off point (usage, engagement, payment, sentiment)
   - Check recent support tickets and escalations
   - Review customer communications (emails, calls, messages)

2. **Root Cause Analysis (1 hour)**
   - Hypothesize root cause (product gap, value misalignment, competitor, budget)
   - Gather additional data (talk to Support, Product, Sales)
   - Validate hypothesis with customer (if unsure)

3. **Engage Customer (1 hour)**
   - Schedule call or meeting (urgent but not panicked)
   - Prepare discussion points based on root cause
   - Conduct call with empathy and curiosity
   - Document findings and action items

4. **Execute Recovery Plan (Variable)**
   - If product gap: Escalate to Product, provide workaround, set timeline
   - If value misalignment: Reframe value, provide success stories, offer training
   - If competitor: Conduct competitive analysis, offer incentives, highlight differentiation
   - If budget: Offer payment plan, downgrade option, pause (last resort)

5. **Monitor and Follow Up (Ongoing)**
   - Update health score daily
   - Follow up on action items within 48 hours
   - Escalate to Head of CS if health score drops below 50 (red)
   - Close playbook when health score ≥70 (green) for 7+ days

**Success Criteria**:
- Health score returns to ≥70 (green) within 14 days
- Customer remains active for 90+ days
- CSAT ≥4 on recovery interaction

**Tools**:
- CRM for health score and customer data
- WhatsApp for outreach
- Support ticketing for issue history

**Dependencies**:
- Product team for feature requests or bug fixes
- Sales team for renewal or expansion negotiations

**Metrics**:
- Playbook execution rate: % of triggered playbooks executed within 24h
- Recovery rate: % of at-risk customers recovered
- Time to recovery: Average days to return to green health

**GRR**: Gross Retention Rate (revenue retained excluding expansion)

**Health Score**: Composite score (0-100) indicating customer health

**NPS**: Net Promoter Score (% Promoters - % Detractors)

**NRR**: Net Revenue Retention (revenue retained including expansion)

**Onboarding**: Initial customer journey from signup to activation (Days 0-14)

**Playbook**: Standardized process for common customer scenarios

**QBR**: Quarterly Business Review (for Scale/Growth customers)

**Renewal**: Customer renews subscription at end of contract term

**Retention**: Customer remains active and continues paying

**TTV**: Time-to-Value (days from signup to first value moment)
