# ConectaPay - Customer Advocacy and Reference Program Design

## Purpose
This document defines the strategy for identifying, nurturing, and leveraging customer advocates to build trust, accelerate sales cycles, and create a self-sustaining growth engine through word-of-mouth.

**Use this document when:**
- Building a customer advocacy program from scratch
- Identifying and recruiting reference customers
- Preparing customers for sales calls, testimonials, or speaking opportunities
- Designing advocate recognition and reward systems
- Measuring advocacy program impact

---

## Executive Summary

### Program Vision
Transform enthusiastic customers into ConectaPay's most powerful marketing asset by building a structured advocacy program that identifies, nurtures, and leverages customer champions at every stage of their journey.

### Core Philosophy
> **"The best salespeople are your happiest customers."**

Customer advocacy is not about incentivizing referrals—that's the Referral Program (CON-88). Advocacy is about identifying customers who are already enthusiastic and giving them opportunities to share their experience authentically.

### Key Differentiators

| Program | Purpose | Incentive | Example |
|---------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Referral Program** | Customer-to-customer recommendations | Financial (R$ 150/100) | "Refer a friend and both get rewards" |
| **Case Studies** | One-off success stories | Recognition/feature | "Success story on website" |
| **Advocacy Program** | Ongoing brand representation | Access, recognition, community | "Speak at event, join advisory board" |

## Advocate Identification Framework

### The Advocacy Pyramid

```
                    ┌─────────────────┐
                    │  ADVISORY BOARD │  (12 customers)
                    │    (Tier 1)     │  Strategic guidance
                    ├─────────────────┤
                    │   CHAMPIONS     │  (30 customers)
                    │    (Tier 2)     │  Active promoters
                    ├─────────────────┤
                    │   ADVOCATES     │  (100+ customers)
                    │    (Tier 3)     │  Willing participants
                    └─────────────────┘
```

### Advocate Identification Signals

#### Tier 3: Advocate Candidates (Top 20% of customers)
**Signals to Identify:**
- **NPS Score**: 9-10 (promoters)
- **Usage**: Top quartile for automation runs, messages sent, revenue recovered
- **Engagement**: High email open rates (>50%), active in support tickets (positive tone)
- **Social**: Positive mentions on social media, tags ConectaPay in posts
- **Referrals**: Has referred 1+ customers (even without program)
- **Feedback**: Submits detailed, constructive feedback

**Screening Questions:**
1. NPS survey: "How likely are you to recommend ConectaPay?" (9-10 = promoter)
2. "Would you be willing to share your experience with other businesses?"
3. "Have you already told others about ConectaPay?"

#### Tier 2: Champions (Top 5% of customers)
**All Tier 3 signals PLUS:**
- **Tenure**: 6+ months as customer
- **ROI**: Quantifiable success story (3x+ ROI, <3 month payback)
- **Proactive**: Reaches out to share ideas, offer help
- **Visible**: Active in industry communities, LinkedIn presence
- **Speaker**: Comfortable speaking to groups (recorded call, video)

**Additional Screening:**
1. Case study interview: "Tell us about your results"
2. Willingness to participate: "Would you be open to a 20-minute call with a prospect?"
3. Availability: "Can you join occasional customer calls or events?"

#### Tier 1: Advisory Board (Top 1% of customers)
**All Tier 2 signals PLUS:**
- **Strategic Mindset**: Can provide product/strategic feedback
- **Industry Authority**: Recognized in their segment (clínicas, imobiliárias)
- **Leadership**: Owns/leads a business with 10+ employees
- **Long-term View**: Committed to ConectaPay partnership (12+ months)
- **Network**: Has connections to other potential customers

**Final Vetting:**
1. In-depth interview: Product vision, industry trends, feedback
2. Alignment with ConectaPay values and mission
3. Conflict of interest check (no competitor relationships)
4. Time commitment: Quarterly 2-hour meetings

## Advocate Recruitment Strategy

### Phase 1: Identification (Ongoing)

**Automated Identification:**
- **NPS Survey**: Automatically flag all 9-10 responses
- **Usage Analytics**: Dashboard of top performers by segment
- **Support Sentiment**: Tag positive sentiment tickets in Zendesk/Intercom
- **Social Listening**: Google Alerts for mentions, LinkedIn tags

**Manual Identification:**
- **CSM Nominations**: CSMs submit advocate candidates monthly
- **Sales Feedback**: Account executives mention enthusiastic prospects
- **Community Activity**: Active participants in webinars, events, forums

**Tracking Tool**: `advocate-pipeline-tracker.csv` (included below)

### Phase 2: Outreach (Personalized)

**Timing**: Reach out 30-60 days after customer achieves key milestone
- First recovered sale
- 3x ROI achieved
- 6-month tenure anniversary
- Positive NPS response

**Outreach Channels:**
1. **Email** (primary): Personal message from CSM or founder
2. **WhatsApp** (follow-up): Quick check-in if no email response
3. **Phone** (high-value): For Tier 1 and 2 candidates
4. **LinkedIn** (relationship building): Connect and engage before ask

### Phase 3: Onboarding (Warm Welcome)

**For All Advocates (Tier 3):**
1. **Welcome Email**: Program overview, benefits, expectations
2. **Advocate Kit**: Digital badge, social media graphics, referral link
3. **Community Invite**: WhatsApp group or Slack channel
4. **First Ask**: Low-friction request (testimonial quote, LinkedIn post)

**For Champions (Tier 2):**
1. **Onboarding Call**: 30-min call to understand goals, availability
2. **Advocate Profile**: Complete profile (bio, photo, success story)
3. **Training Session**: "How to be a Great Advocate" (optional)
4. **Calendar Invite**: Add to advocate calendar for upcoming opportunities

**For Advisory Board (Tier 1):**
1. **Welcome Package**: Physical gift (ConectaPay swag, handwritten note)
2. **Onboarding Session**: 60-min deep dive on program expectations
3. **First Meeting**: Schedule quarterly advisory board meeting
4. **Access Grant**: Private Slack channel, direct founder access

## Reference Program Integration

### Sales Reference Process

**When a Prospect Requests a Reference:**

1. **Identify Match Criteria**:
   - Segment (clínica vs imobiliária)
   - Business size (similar revenue/employee count)
   - Geography (same city/region if possible)
   - Use case (similar pain points/goals)

2. **Check Advocate Availability**:
   - Review `advocate-availability-tracker.csv`
   - Filter by segment, recent activity, availability
   - Prioritize Tier 2 (Champions) first

3. **Make Warm Introduction**:
   - **To Prospect**: "I'd like to connect you with [Advocate Name], who runs [Business Type] in [City]. They've seen [Result] with ConectaPay and can share their experience."
   - **To Advocate**: "Hi [Name], we have a prospect in [Segment] looking at ConectaPay. Would you be open to a 20-min call to share your experience? Here's some context about them."

4. **Prepare Both Sides**:
   - **Prospect**: Background on advocate, their business, results
   - **Advocate**: Prospect's context, key questions to address, talking points
   - **Send Brief**: One-page brief with discussion points

5. **Facilitate Call**:
   - Option A: ConectaPay team member introduces on call (5 min intro, then leaves)
   - Option B: Direct email/WhatsApp intro, advocates connect directly

6. **Follow Up**:
   - **To Prospect**: "How was the call with [Advocate]? Any other questions?"
   - **To Advocate**: Thank you note, account credit, update on outcome

### Reference Call Brief Template

```
REFERENCE CALL BRIEF

**Advocate**: [Name, Business, Location]
**Prospect**: [Name, Business, Location]
**Date Scheduled**: [Date/Time]

**Advocate Background:**
- Business: [Type, size, specialty]
- Results with ConectaPay: [Key metrics]
- Time as customer: [X months]
- Best topics to discuss: [Automation, ROI, implementation, etc.]

**Prospect Context:**
- Main pain points: [No-shows, lead conversion, etc.]
- Concerns/objections: [Price, implementation, results]
- Questions for advocate: [List 3-5 specific questions]

**Discussion Guide:**
1. "Tell me about your business before ConectaPay"
2. "What problem were you trying to solve?"
3. "How did you implement ConectaPay?"
4. "What results have you seen?"
5. "What would you say to someone considering ConectaPay?"

**Post-Call:**
- Debrief with advocate (send notes/feedback)
- Follow up with prospect
- Log interaction in CRM
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Treating Advocacy Like Referrals
**Problem**: Offering financial incentives for advocacy activities
**Solution**: Advocacy is about enthusiasm and relationship, not transaction
**Fix**: Focus on recognition, access, and community—not cash

### ❌ Mistake 2: Over-Committing Advocates
**Problem**: Asking too much, too soon from new advocates
**Solution**: Start small, build trust, increase asks gradually
**Fix**: First ask should be <15 minutes (testimonial quote, quick feedback)

### ❌ Mistake 3: One-Way Communication
**Problem**: Only reaching out when you need something
**Solution**: Provide value between asks
**Fix**: Share product updates, offer exclusives, celebrate wins

### ❌ Mistake 4: Ignoring Advocate Feedback
**Problem**: Collecting feedback but not acting on it
**Solution**: Close the loop with every piece of feedback
**Fix**: "You suggested X, we built Y" updates

### ❌ Mistake 5: Poor Matching for Sales References
**Problem**: Connecting prospects with mismatched advocates
**Solution**: Careful screening by segment, size, use case
**Fix**: Detailed matching criteria in reference brief

### ❌ Mistake 6: No Preparation for Reference Calls
**Problem**: Advocates don't know what to expect on calls
**Solution**: Always provide brief and talking points
**Fix**: Send reference call brief 24 hours in advance

### ❌ Mistake 7: Forgetting to Say Thank You
**Problem**: Advocates complete activities but get no acknowledgment
**Solution**: Immediate gratitude after every activity
**Fix**: Automated thank you email + personal note from founder

### ❌ Mistake 8: Neglecting the Community
**Problem**: Advocates don't know each other
**Solution**: Foster advocate-to-advocate connections
**Fix**: WhatsApp group, virtual events, in-person meetups

## Legal & Compliance Considerations

### Advocate Participation Agreement

**For All Advocates (Tier 2 & 3):**
- Permission to use name, photo, logo in marketing materials
- Permission to share quotes and testimonials publicly
- Right to review and approve case studies before publication
- No exclusivity requirement (can use competitors)
- Can withdraw from program at any time

**For Advisory Board (Tier 1):**
- Non-disclosure agreement (NDA) for confidential product discussions
- Non-compete clause (cannot work with direct competitors during term)
- Code of conduct (professional behavior, respect confidentiality)
- Compensation terms (if applicable)
- Term length (1 year, renewable)

### Data Privacy (LGPD Compliance)

- **Consent**: Explicit consent to collect and use advocate data
- **Purpose**: Clearly state how data will be used
- **Access**: Advocates can request data access or deletion
- **Security**: Protect advocate information (encrypted storage)
- **Sharing**: No data sharing with third parties without consent

### Incentive Disclosure

**For Financial Incentives:**
- Disclose relationship when advocate provides testimonial
- Example: "[Name] is a ConectaPay customer and advocate"
- No need to disclose non-monetary recognition (badges, features)
