## Executive Summary

This playbook defines the white-glove customer success methodology for ConectaPay's high-touch customer segments. High-touch customers (enterprise and high-value SMBs) require a strategic partnership approach, not just transactional support.

**High-Touch Segment Definition**:
- **Enterprise**: R$ 5,000+ ARR, multi-location, complex integrations, executive sponsorship
- **High-Value SMB**: R$ 1,000-5,000 ARR, growth-oriented, expansion potential

**Core Philosophy**: Strategic partnership over transactional support. We become an extension of their team, invested in their business outcomes.

**Key Differentiators from Standard CS**:
| Dimension | Standard CS | High-Touch CS |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **CSM:Cust Ratio** | 1:200 | 1:25-50 |
| **Touch Frequency** | Monthly/Bi-weekly | Weekly/Bi-weekly |
| **Reviews** | Annual QBR | Quarterly EBR + Monthly check-ins |
| **Onboarding** | Self-serve + 1 call | Dedicated 4-week white-glove program |
| **Support** | Pool/shared | Dedicated success manager + priority queue |
| **Strategic Planning** | Ad-hoc | Co-created success plans |

**Success Objectives**:
- Achieve 95%+ retention (vs. 90% standard)
- Drive 30%+ expansion revenue (vs. 20% standard)
- Maintain 80+ NPS (vs. 70+ standard)
- Generate 50%+ referral rate (vs. 40% standard)
- Create 15+ case studies annually from high-touch customers

## 1. High-Touch Customer Identification & Segmentation

### 1.1 High-Touch Qualification Criteria

**Automatic Qualification** (meet ANY criteria):
- R$ 5,000+ ARR (Enterprise)
- R$ 1,000+ ARR with growth trajectory (High-Value SMB)
- Multi-location or multi-business unit
- Executive sponsorship (C-level, VP, Director)
- Complex integration requirements
- Referenceable potential (industry leader, brand recognition)

**Manual Qualification** (for edge cases):
Customer Success Director approval for customers who:
- Show strong expansion potential (growing 20%+ QoQ)
- Are strategic market influencers
- Provide access to partnership opportunities

### 1.2 High-Touch Tier Structure

| Tier | ARR Range | CSM Ratio | EBR Frequency | Support Level | Onboarding |
|------|-----------|-----------|---------------|---------------|------------|
| **Enterprise** | R$ 5k+/mo | 1:25 | Quarterly | Dedicated + Slack | 4-week white-glove |
| **High-Value SMB** | R$ 1k-5k/mo | 1:50 | Semi-annually | Priority queue | 2-week accelerated |
| **Standard SMB** | R$ 297-997/mo | 1:200 | Annual | Shared support | Self-serve |

### 1.3 Segment-Specific High-Touch Profiles

#### Profile 1: Multi-Location Clinic Chains
**Characteristics**:
- 3+ clinic locations
- R$ 10,000+ ARR
- 20+ staff members
- Complex scheduling integration needs

**Success Priorities**:
1. Standardize automation across all locations
2. Centralized reporting and ROI tracking
3. Staff training and adoption programs
4. Multi-location expansion support

**CSM Approach**: Quarterly in-person business reviews, centralized success plan, location-specific optimization

#### Profile 2: High-Volume Real Estate Brokerages
**Characteristics**:
- 50+ agents
- R$ 5,000+ ARR
- 1,000+ leads/month
- CRM integration required

**Success Priorities**:
1. Lead response time optimization (<5 min)
2. Agent adoption and training
3. Integration with existing CRM/tools
4. Commission tracking and ROI attribution

**CSM Approach**: Monthly check-ins, quarterly EBRs, agent training workshops, custom integration support

#### Profile 3: Fast-Growing Single-Location Businesses
**Characteristics**:
- Single location but rapid growth
- R$ 1,000-2,500 ARR growing to R$ 3,000+
- Expansion plans within 6 months
- High executive engagement

**Success Priorities**:
1. Scale automation with growth
2. Prepare for multi-location expansion
3. Proactive feature adoption
4. Strategic partnership for growth

**CSM Approach**: Bi-weekly check-ins, growth planning sessions, expansion roadmap co-creation

### 1.4 High-Touch Customer Health Score

**Enhanced Health Scoring** (150 points total):

| Dimension | Weight | High-Touch Specific Metrics |
|-----------|--------|-----------------------------|
| **Strategic Alignment** | 20% | Success plan progress, executive engagement, goal alignment |
| **Product Engagement** | 25% | Multi-location adoption, feature depth, integration usage |
| **Value Realization** | 30% | ROI achieved, revenue impact, efficiency gains |
| **Relationship Strength** | 15% | Executive sponsor relationship, communication quality |
| **Growth Trajectory** | 10% | Expansion signals, referral activity, advocacy |

**Health Tiers** (High-Touch Specific):
- **PLATINUM** (125-150): Strategic partner, case study candidate, advisory board
- **GOLD** (100-124): Healthy, expansion ready, advocate potential
- **SILVER** (75-99): On track, monitor for growth opportunities
- **BRONZE** (50-74): At-risk, intervention required
- **CRITICAL** (0-49): Executive engagement needed

## 2. White-Glove Onboarding Experience

### 2.1 White-Glove Onboarding Philosophy

**Standard vs. High-Touch Onboarding**:

| Dimension | Standard Onboarding | White-Glove Onboarding |
|-----------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Duration** | 14 days | 28 days (Enterprise) / 14 days (High-Value SMB) |
| **Owner** | Shared CSM | Dedicated Success Manager |
| **Touchpoints** | 3 calls + email | 10+ touchpoints (calls, WhatsApp, in-person) |
| **Customization** | Templates | Tailored to business goals |
| **Training** | Recorded videos | Live training sessions + documentation |
| **Success Plan** | Generic goals | Co-created strategic success plan |
| **Time-to-Value** | 7 days | 3 days (accelerated via dedicated support) |

### 2.2 Enterprise Onboarding Journey (28 Days)

#### Phase 1: Discovery & Planning (Days 0-3)

**Day 0: Welcome & Executive Alignment**
- Executive sponsor welcome call (30 min)
- CSM introduction and success manager assignment
- Access to dedicated Slack channel (for day-to-day)
- Overview of white-glove onboarding process

**Day 1-2: Business Discovery**
- Deep-dive discovery session (60 min)
  - Business goals and objectives (6-12 month horizon)
  - Current tech stack and integration needs
  - Pain points and success criteria
  - Stakeholder mapping (who needs to be involved)
- Technical requirements assessment
- Integration planning with existing systems

**Day 3: Success Plan Co-Creation**
- Present draft success plan based on discovery
- Align on KPIs and success metrics
- Set milestones for 30, 60, 90 days
- Identify quick wins (first 7 days)
- Executive sponsor sign-off

**Deliverables**:
- Business Discovery Document
- Technical Integration Plan
- Co-created Success Plan (30-60-90 day roadmap)

#### Phase 2: Foundation & Integration (Days 4-10)

**Day 4-5: Technical Setup**
- Dedicated technical setup session
- WhatsApp Business API configuration (all locations)
- Integration deployment (CRM, ERP, scheduling systems)
- Data migration and import support
- Custom configuration based on business needs

**Day 6-7: Team Training Session 1**
- Live training for key stakeholders (60-90 min)
  - Admin users: Full platform training
  - Staff users: Day-to-day usage training
  - Executive sponsors: Dashboard and reporting
- Custom training materials (business-specific use cases)
- Q&A session

**Day 8-10: Launch & First Automation**
- Go-live with first automation campaign
- Real-time monitoring and support
- Optimization of first campaign based on results
- Celebration of first wins

**Deliverables**:
- Fully integrated system
- Trained team members
- First live automation campaign
- Initial results report

#### Phase 3: Optimization & Expansion (Days 11-21)

**Day 11-14: Performance Review**
- First-week performance review call
- Analyze initial results and optimize
- Address any technical issues or friction
- Identify expansion opportunities

**Day 15: Team Training Session 2**
- Advanced features training
- Best practices for ongoing optimization
- A/B testing framework introduction
- Reporting and dashboard deep-dive

**Day 16-21: Expansion Planning**
- Rollout to additional locations/business units
- Advanced automation sequences
- Integration optimization
- Prepare for Day 30 success review

**Deliverables**:
- Optimized campaigns
- Expanded usage (multi-location/advanced features)
- Team adoption metrics

#### Phase 4: Value Realization & Transition (Days 22-28)

**Day 22-25: 30-Day Success Preparation**
- Compile 30-day impact report
- Calculate ROI achieved
- Gather testimonials and feedback
- Prepare transition to ongoing success management

**Day 26-27: Executive Business Review Preparation**
- Draft EBR presentation
- Review success plan progress
- Identify expansion opportunities
- Schedule first EBR (Day 60)

**Day 28: Onboarding Completion**
- Onboarding completion celebration
- Transition to ongoing cadence established
- Success plan updated for Day 30-90
- Ongoing support channels confirmed

**Deliverables**:
- 30-day impact report
- Testimonials (if applicable)
- Updated success plan
- EBR scheduled

### 2.3 High-Value SMB Onboarding (14 Days)

**Accelerated White-Glove Program**:

| Day | Activity | Owner | Duration |
|-----|----------|-------|----------|
| 0 | Welcome call + success manager assignment | CSM | 30 min |

- ✅ 100% complete onboarding (no drop-off)
- ✅ All locations/business units live by Day 21
- ✅ Success plan signed by executive sponsor
- ✅ First value achieved by Day 7

## 3. Strategic Account Management

### 3.1 The Strategic Partnership Model

**High-Touch CSM as Strategic Partner**:

| Traditional CSM Role | Strategic Partner Role |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| Reactive support | Proactive consultation |
| Product training | Business outcome optimization |
| Issue resolution | Growth and expansion planning |
| Quarterly check-ins | Strategic alignment sessions |

### 3.2 Stakeholder Mapping & Management

**Identify Key Stakeholders**:

| Stakeholder Type | Role | Engagement Strategy |
|------------------|------|---------------------|
| **Executive Sponsor** | Budget, strategy, ROI | Quarterly EBRs, ad-hoc strategic updates |

- Executive Sponsor: Quarterly (EBR) + ad-hoc for major updates

### 3.3 Communication Cadence

**High-Value SMB Customers**:

| Frequency | Touchpoint Type | Owner | Duration | Participants |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|----------|--------------|

| **Weekly** | Email/WhatsApp async | CSM | Ongoing | CSM + Owner/Manager |
| **Monthly** | Success plan review | CSM | 30 min | CSM + Owner/Manager |
| **Semi-annually** | Business Review | CSM | 45 min | CSM + Owner/Manager |
| **Ad-hoc** | Expansion planning | CSM | 30 min | CSM + Owner/Manager |

### 3.4 Proactive Account Management

**Weekly Account Review Process** (CSM Internal):

1. **Health Score Review** (10 min)
   - Check for health score changes (>5 point shift)
   - Review usage metrics (logins, messages, campaigns)
   - Flag any red/yellow indicators

2. **Success Plan Progress** (10 min)
   - Review milestones due this week
   - Identify blockers or delays
   - Plan interventions

3. **Expansion Opportunities** (5 min)
   - Check for expansion triggers (volume growth, new locations)
   - Identify upsell opportunities
   - Plan expansion conversations

4. **Risk Assessment** (5 min)
   - Review support tickets and sentiment
   - Check for competitor mentions
   - Assess churn risk

5. **Weekly Prioritization** (5 min)
   - Rank accounts by priority (Platinum > Gold > Silver > Bronze)
   - Plan weekly touchpoints
   - Schedule proactive interventions

### 3.5 Account Planning & Governance

**Quarterly Account Planning** (Internal CSM + CS Director):

**Purpose**: Strategic planning for each high-touch account

**Agenda**:
1. Review past quarter performance (health score, NPS, expansion)
2. Identify strategic opportunities for next quarter
3. Plan EBR approach and key messages
4. Assess risk and mitigation strategies
5. Set objectives for next quarter

**Output**: Quarterly Account Plan for each high-touch customer

## 4. Executive Business Reviews (EBRs)

### 4.1 EBR vs. Standard QBR

| Dimension | Standard QBR | Executive Business Review (EBR) |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------------------|
| **Audience** | Manager/Owner | C-level, VP, Director |
| **Focus** | Product usage and ROI | Strategic alignment and business outcomes |
| **Duration** | 30-45 min | 60-90 min |
| **Preparation** | 1-2 days | 1 week |
| **Content** | Metrics dashboard | Strategic roadmap + business impact |
| **Outcome** | Renewal discussion | Strategic planning + expansion roadmap |
| **Attendees** | CSM + Customer | CSM + CS Director + Executive Sponsor |

### 4.2 EBR Cadence

| Customer Tier | Frequency | Duration | Preparation Time |
|---------------|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| Enterprise | Quarterly | 90 min | 1 week |
| High-Value SMB | Semi-annually | 60 min | 3 days |

### 4.3 Pre-EBR Preparation (1 Week Before)

**Day 7: Data Collection**
- Pull customer metrics (90 days)
- Calculate ROI and business impact
- Gather usage analytics across all locations
- Compile support ticket summary
- Review NPS and feedback

**Day 6-5: Analysis & Insights**
- Identify trends and patterns
- Calculate year-over-year growth
- Benchmark against similar customers
- Identify expansion opportunities
- Prepare competitive analysis (if applicable)

**Day 4-3: Presentation Development**
- Create EBR slide deck (15-20 slides)
- Develop executive summary (1-page)
- Prepare success plan progress report
- Create expansion roadmap (if applicable)
- Draft recommendations

**Day 2: Internal Review**
- Review with CS Director
- Refine messaging and recommendations
- Practice presentation
- Prepare for objections and questions

**Day 1: Send Pre-EBR Materials**
- Send EBR agenda to customer
- Share pre-read materials (executive summary, metrics)
- Confirm attendance and logistics
- Adjust agenda based on customer priorities

### 4.4 EBR Agenda Template (90 Minutes)

**Section 1: Welcome & Executive Check-In** (5 min)
- Personal connection
- Business updates since last review
- Agenda review and adjustments

**Section 2: Executive Summary** (10 min)
- Top 3 wins from the quarter
- Health score and NPS snapshot
- ROI achieved (quantified business impact)
- Strategic alignment status

**Section 3: Deep Dive: Business Impact** (15 min)
- Revenue recovered/attributed to ConectaPay
- Efficiency gains (time saved, staff productivity)
- Multi-location performance breakdown
- Key success stories and testimonials

**Section 4: Success Plan Review** (10 min)
- Previous quarter goals: status and achievements
- Milestones completed vs. planned
- Gaps and lessons learned
- Success plan evolution: what's changing

**Section 5: Strategic Alignment** (15 min)
- Customer's business goals for next quarter
- How ConectaPay aligns with strategic priorities
- Industry trends and opportunities
- Competitive landscape insights

**Section 6: Expansion Roadmap** (15 min)
- Growth opportunities identified
- Expansion recommendations (tier upgrade, new locations, features)
- ROI projection for expansion scenarios
- Timeline and implementation plan

**Section 7: Product Roadmap Preview** (10 min)
- Upcoming features relevant to customer
- Beta program invitations
- Strategic input requests (customer feedback)

**Section 8: Feedback & Discussion** (5 min)
- Open floor for questions and concerns
- Strategic feedback from executive sponsor
- Suggestions for ConectaPay improvement

**Section 9: Next Steps & Commitments** (5 min)
- Action items (with owners and deadlines)
- Next EBR date scheduled
- Commitments from both parties

### 4.5 EBR Slide Deck Structure (15-20 Slides)

1. **Title Slide**: Customer name, date, quarter/year, attendees
2. **Executive Summary**: 3 key wins, ROI snapshot, health score
3. **Business Impact Dashboard**: Revenue recovered, efficiency gains, growth metrics
4. **Success Stories**: 2-3 specific wins with screenshots/testimonials
5. **Multi-Location Performance**: Breakdown by location/business unit (if applicable)
6. **Success Plan Progress**: Previous goals vs. actuals, milestones achieved
7. **Strategic Alignment**: Customer goals and ConectaPay fit
8. **Expansion Opportunities**: Personalized recommendations with ROI projections
9. **Expansion Roadmap**: Timeline and implementation plan
10. **Product Roadmap**: Upcoming features relevant to customer
11. **Benchmarking**: How customer compares to top performers
12. **Year-Over-Year Growth**: Trends and trajectory
13. **Feedback Summary**: NPS, support tickets, suggestions
14. **Next Quarter Goals**: Proposed success plan updates
15. **Action Items**: Clear next steps with owners and deadlines
16. **Thank You**: Contact info, next EBR date, appreciation

### 4.6 Post-EBR Actions

**Within 24 Hours**:
- Send thank-you email with:
  - EBR presentation PDF
  - Action items summary
  - Next EBR date and logistics
  - Appreciation for executive sponsor's time

**Within 7 Days**:
- Check on action item progress
- Address any outstanding questions
- Send additional materials or follow-up information

**Within 30 Days**:
- Mini check-in on goals
- Monitor action item completion
- Prepare for monthly success plan review

### 4.7 EBR Success Metrics

**Engagement Metrics**:
- 95%+ attendance rate from executive sponsors
- 90%+ of EBRs completed on schedule
- 85%+ action items completed within 30 days

**Outcome Metrics**:
- 30%+ of EBRs result in expansion conversation

## 5. Success Planning Framework

### 5.1 What is a Success Plan?

A **Success Plan** is a co-created strategic roadmap that aligns ConectaPay's capabilities with the customer's business objectives. Unlike generic goals, success plans are specific, measurable, and tied to business outcomes.

**Standard Goals vs. Success Plans**:

| Dimension | Standard Goals | Success Plans |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| **Origin** | ConectaPay template | Co-created with customer |
| **Focus** | Product usage | Business outcomes |
| **Timeline** | 30-60-90 days | 6-12 months |
| **Measurement** | Feature adoption | ROI and business impact |
| **Ownership** | CSM-owned | Joint ownership |
| **Flexibility** | Static | Evolving with business needs |

### 5.2 Success Plan Framework

**Success Plan Components**:

1. **Business Objectives** (Customer-defined)
   - What are the customer's strategic goals? (revenue growth, efficiency, expansion)
   - Timeline: 6-12 months

2. **ConectaPay Alignment** (Co-created)
   - How does ConectaPay enable these objectives?
   - Specific features and use cases

3. **Key Results** (Measurable outcomes)
   - Quantifiable metrics (ROI, revenue recovered, time saved)
   - Targets and timelines

4. **Milestones** (Progress markers)
   - 30, 60, 90-day checkpoints
   - Success criteria for each phase

5. **Actions & Owners** (Execution plan)
   - Specific tasks with owners and deadlines
   - Shared responsibilities (ConectaPay + Customer)

6. **Risks & Mitigation** (Contingency planning)
   - Potential blockers
   - Mitigation strategies

### 5.3 Success Plan Template

**Section 1: Business Objectives**

| Objective | Why This Matters | Timeline | Success Metric |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|----------------|
| Example: Reduce no-shows by 50% | Recovers R$ 10,000/month in lost revenue | 6 months | No-show rate < 12% |
| Example: Scale to 5 locations | Supports expansion strategy | 12 months | 5 locations live on ConectaPay |
| Example: Increase lead conversion by 30% | Drives R$ 25,000/month in new revenue | 3 months | Conversion rate > 8% |

**Section 2: ConectaPay Alignment**

| Business Objective | ConectaPay Solution | Implementation |
|-------------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Reduce no-shows | Automated appointment reminders + recovery sequences | Deploy 3-automation sequence (24h before, same day, 48h after) |
| Scale to 5 locations | Multi-location management + centralized reporting | Rollout to 2 new locations per quarter |
| Increase lead conversion | Instant response + automated follow-up sequences | Implement 5-touch sequence (instant, 1h, 24h, 72h, 7d) |

**Section 3: Key Results & Milestones**

**30-Day Milestones**:
- [ ] All locations live on ConectaPay
- [ ] 3 core automation sequences deployed
- [ ] Team trained and adopting platform
- [ ] First value achieved: R$ X recovered
- [ ] ROI achieved: >100%

**60-Day Milestones**:
- [ ] Optimization complete: sequences refined based on data
- [ ] Advanced features deployed (A/B testing, segmentation)
- [ ] Multi-location reporting centralized
- [ ] ROI achieved: >200%
- [ ] Expansion opportunity identified

**90-Day Milestones**:
- [ ] Success plan fully executed
- [ ] Business objectives on track
- [ ] NPS ≥ 8
- [ ] Case study candidate qualified
- [ ] Expansion roadmap defined

**Section 4: Action Plan**

| Action | Owner | Deadline | Status | Dependencies |
|--------|-------|----------|--------|--------------|
| Deploy appointment reminders | Customer (Admin) | Day 7 | Complete | None |
| Train staff on platform | CSM + Customer Champion | Day 14 | In Progress | Admin trained first |
| Integrate with CRM | Technical Team | Day 21 | Pending | Customer API access |
| Create expansion roadmap | CSM + Executive Sponsor | Day 60 | Pending | 90-day review |

**Section 5: Risks & Mitigation**

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation | Owner |
|------|--------|-------------|------------|-------|
| Staff adoption resistance | High | Medium | Training + change management support | CSM |
| Integration delays | Medium | Low | Dedicated technical support | Technical Lead |
| Budget constraints for expansion | High | Low | ROI justification + flexible pricing | CSM + CS Director |

### 5.4 Success Plan Creation Process

**Step 1: Discovery** (Day 0-1)
- Conduct deep-dive business discovery
- Understand strategic objectives
- Identify stakeholders and decision makers
- Map current state and desired future state

**Step 2: Draft Success Plan** (Day 2-3)
- Translate objectives into ConectaPay alignment
- Define key results and milestones
- Identify actions, owners, and timelines
- Assess risks and mitigation strategies

**Step 3: Review & Refine** (Day 4-7)
- Present draft to customer for feedback
- Co-create and refine together
- Align on metrics and timelines
- Get executive sponsor sign-off

**Step 4: Launch & Track** (Day 8+)
- Execute action plan
- Track progress against milestones
- Review and adjust at regular intervals
- Celebrate wins and course-correct as needed

### 5.5 Success Plan Review Cadence

| Review Type | Frequency | Purpose | Participants |
|-------------|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **Weekly Check-in** | Weekly | Progress tracking, blocker removal | CSM + Champion |
| **Monthly Review** | Monthly | Milestone assessment, plan adjustment | CSM + Executive Sponsor (optional) |
| **Quarterly EBR** | Quarterly | Strategic alignment, expansion planning | CSM + CS Director + Executive Sponsor |
| **Annual Refresh** | Annually | Success plan renewal for next year | CSM + CS Director + Executive Sponsor |

## 6. Proactive Success Management

### 6.1 Proactive vs. Reactive Success

| Dimension | Reactive Success | Proactive Success |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Trigger** | Customer reaches out with issue | Data-driven signals identify opportunity/risk |
| **Timing** | After problem occurs | Before problem escalates |
| **Focus** | Issue resolution | Value creation and optimization |
| **Outcome** | Problem solved | Customer delight + expansion |

### 6.2 Proactive Health Monitoring

**Daily Health Signals** (automated monitoring):
- Health score changes (>5 point shift)
- Login frequency changes
- Usage spikes or drops
- Support ticket sentiment analysis
- Payment issues

**Weekly Health Review** (CSM manual review):
- Portfolio health score trends
- Success plan progress
- Expansion trigger identification
- Risk assessment

**Monthly Health Deep-Dive** (CSM + CS Director):
- Health score accuracy review
- At-risk account intervention planning
- Expansion opportunity prioritization
- Strategic account planning

### 6.3 Proactive Intervention Triggers

**Trigger 1: Health Score Decline** (>5 point drop)
**Action**: Within 24 hours
- CSM outreach to understand root cause
- Identify friction points or blockers
- Offer support and resources
- Update success plan if needed

**Trigger 2: Usage Plateau** (no growth for 30 days)
**Action**: Within 48 hours
- Optimization session
- Identify new use cases or features
- A/B testing recommendations
- Success plan refresh

**Trigger 3: Executive Sponsor Disengagement** (no response in 2 weeks)
**Action**: Within 7 days
- CS Director outreach
- Relationship rebuild
- Strategic value reinforcement
- Success plan realignment

**Trigger 4: Expansion Signal** (volume growth >30%, new location)
**Action**: Within 3 days
- Expansion opportunity call
- ROI projection preparation
- Expansion roadmap development
- Proposal presentation

**Trigger 5: Champion Departure** (key contact leaves company)
**Action**: Within 24 hours
- Relationship transfer protocol
- New champion identification and onboarding
- Success plan continuity
- Executive sponsor re-engagement

### 6.4 Proactive Value Creation

**Value Creation Opportunities**:

| Opportunity | Signal | Action | Expected Outcome |
|-------------|--------|--------|------------------|
| **Optimization** | Usage plateau or underperformance | Optimization session, A/B testing | +20-30% performance improvement |
| **New Feature Adoption** | New feature launch relevant to customer | Personalized feature demo + use case | Increased feature depth, higher ROI |
| **Best Practice Sharing** | Customer not using industry best practices | Share benchmarking data, case studies | Improved performance, competitive advantage |
| **Strategic Planning** | Customer growth phase or expansion | Strategic planning session | Aligned roadmaps, expansion opportunities |
| **Efficiency Gains** | Manual workarounds identified | Process automation, workflow optimization | Time savings, staff productivity |

### 6.5 The Proactive Success Cycle

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    PROACTIVE SUCCESS CYCLE                   │
│                                                              │
│  1. MONITOR → 2. IDENTIFY → 3. PRIORITIZE → 4. ACT → 5. MEASURE │
│      ↑                                                           │
│      └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
│                              │                                  │
│                         LEARN & ITERATE                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Step 1: Monitor** (Continuous)
- Automated health monitoring
- Usage analytics
- Sentiment analysis
- Market and competitive intelligence

**Step 2: Identify** (Weekly)
- Review signals and triggers
- Identify opportunities and risks
- Categorize by type and urgency

**Step 3: Prioritize** (Weekly)
- Rank by impact and effort
- Focus on high-impact, quick-win opportunities
- Balance proactive interventions across portfolio

**Step 4: Act** (Daily/Weekly)
- Execute interventions
- Outreach to customers
- Provide value and support

**Step 5: Measure** (Monthly)
- Track intervention outcomes
- Measure impact on health score, NPS, expansion
- Learn and iterate

**Step 6: Learn & Iterate** (Quarterly)
- Analyze what worked and what didn't
- Refine triggers and intervention playbooks
- Improve proactive success capabilities

## 7. Expansion Opportunity Identification

### 7.1 Expansion Types

**Type 1: Tier Upgrades**
- Starter → Pro (R$ 297 → R$ 497/month)
- Pro → Enterprise (custom pricing)
- Trigger: Volume growth, feature requests

**Type 2: Seat/Location Expansion**
- Add new users/locations
- Multi-location packages
- Trigger: Business growth, new locations

**Type 3: Volume Expansion**
- Higher message tiers
- Increased automation volume
- Trigger: Usage growth (80%+ of limit)

**Type 4: Add-on Products**
- New features (beta access, premium integrations)
- Advanced analytics and reporting
- Trigger: Feature requests, maturity

**Type 5: Annual Commitments**
- Monthly → Annual (12-month upfront)
- Multi-year commitments (2-3 years)
- Trigger: Renewal, budget cycles

### 7.2 Expansion Signals

**High-Confidence Signals** (80%+ close probability):
- Message volume at 90%+ of limit for 2+ months
- New location or business unit opened
- Executive sponsor asks about enterprise features
- Competitor comparison (evaluating upgrades vs. switching)
- Budget available for Q2/Q3 planning

**Medium-Confidence Signals** (50%+ close probability):
- Message volume growth >30% QoQ
- Team expansion (hiring new staff)
- Integration requests (ERP, CRM)
- Feature requests for premium capabilities
- Positive NPS with expansion feedback

**Low-Confidence Signals** (20%+ close probability):
- General satisfaction with platform
- Attendance at training sessions
- Engagement with customer community
- Referral behavior

### 7.3 Expansion Qualification Framework

**Qualification Scorecard** (score 0-100):

| Dimension | Weight | Criteria | Points |
|-----------|--------|----------|--------|
| **Value Realized** | 30% | ROI achieved | 0-30 |
| **Growth Trajectory** | 25% | Business growth signals | 0-25 |
| **Executive Sponsorship** | 20% | Executive engagement | 0-20 |
| **Budget Availability** | 15% | Budget timing and amount | 0-15 |
| **Strategic Fit** | 10% | Expansion alignment with goals | 0-10 |

**Qualification Tiers**:
- **80-100 points**: High-priority expansion opportunity (pursue immediately)
- **60-79 points**: Medium-priority (add to pipeline, pursue in 30-60 days)
- **40-59 points**: Low-priority (monitor and nurture)
- **<40 points**: Not qualified (continue nurturing, no expansion outreach)

### 7.4 Expansion Outreach Playbook

**Step 1: Preparation** (2-3 days before outreach)
- Pull customer metrics and ROI data
- Identify expansion opportunity
- Prepare ROI projection for expansion scenario
- Create personalized business case

**Step 2: Outreach** (email or call)
**Expansion Email Template**:

> Assunto: Uma oportunidade de crescimento para [Customer Name] 🚀
>
> Olá [Executive Sponsor Name],
>
> Estou analisando seus resultados com a ConectaPay e fiquei impressionado.
>
> **Seu impacto até agora**:
> - R$ [X] recuperados em vendas perdidas
> - [Y]% de redução em no-shows (ou [Y]% de aumento em conversões)
> - ROI de [Z]%
>
> **Oportunidade identificada**:
> Com base no seu crescimento atual [describe growth signal], vejo uma oportunidade para [expansion type: aumentar volume / adicionar localidade / upgrade de plano].
>
> **Projeção de impacto**:
> Se expandir para [expansion scenario], projetamos:
> - R$ [Projected additional revenue] recuperados por mês
> - ROI total de [Total ROI]%
> - Payback em [X] meses
>
> Posso apresentar os detalhes em uma ligação de 15 minutos esta semana?
>
> Abraço,
> [CSM Name]

**Step 3: Expansion Call** (15-30 minutes)
**Agenda**:
1. Celebrate current success (5 min)
2. Present expansion opportunity and ROI projection (10 min)
3. Address questions and objections (5-10 min)
4. Propose next steps and timeline (5 min)

**Step 4: Proposal & Closing** (within 48 hours)
- Send formal proposal with ROI breakdown
- Offer limited-time incentive (10% discount for annual, etc.)
- Follow up in 3 days
- Close within 14 days

### 7.5 Expansion Incentives

**Common Incentives** (use strategically):

| Incentive Type | When to Use | Discount |
|----------------|-------------|----------|
| **Annual Commitment** | Monthly customer, renewal | 2 months free (17% discount) |
| **Multi-Year** | High-confidence, enterprise | 10-20% discount + price lock |
| **Early Adopter** | Beta features, new products | 20-50% discount for feedback |
| **Volume Prepay** | High-volume customer | 10% discount for 12-month prepay |
| **Bundle** | Multiple expansions | 15% discount for bundle vs. individual |

**Incentive Guidelines**:
- Always tie incentives to commitment (annual, multi-year, prepay)
- Don't discount without business justification
- Get CS Director approval for discounts >20%
- Document rationale for all incentives

## 8. Crisis Management & Escalation

### 8.1 Crisis Definition

A **crisis** is a situation that threatens the customer relationship and requires immediate executive-level intervention.

**Crisis Indicators**:
- Health score drops below 50 (Critical tier)
- Competitor mentioned with serious intent to switch
- Executive sponsor disengaged or unresponsive
- Service outage or critical bug affecting operations
- Payment delinquency or contract dispute
- Public relations issue (negative feedback, social media)

### 8.2 Crisis Response Protocol

**Phase 1: Identification** (within 1 hour)
- Crisis signal detected
- Assess severity and impact
- Determine crisis level (1, 2, or 3)

**Crisis Levels**:
- **Level 1 (Severe)**: Immediate churn risk, executive escalation required
- **Level 2 (High)**: At-risk, CS Director intervention needed
- **Level 3 (Medium)**: Concerning, CSM can manage with support

**Phase 2: Mobilization** (within 2 hours for Level 1)
- Assemble crisis response team:
  - CS Director (lead)
  - CSM (customer relationship owner)
  - Technical Lead (if technical issue)
  - Product Manager (if product issue)
  - Executive Sponsor (ConectaPay CEO/CRO for Level 1)

**Phase 3: Response** (within 24 hours)
- Executive outreach to customer executive sponsor
- Root cause analysis
- Action plan development
- Immediate remediation (if applicable)

**Phase 4: Resolution** (24-72 hours)
- Execute action plan
- Daily check-ins until resolved
- Document resolution and lessons learned

**Phase 5: Recovery** (1-4 weeks)
- Rebuild relationship
- Demonstrate value
- Prevent recurrence
- Monitor for residual issues

### 8.3 Crisis Communication Templates

**Level 1 Crisis: Executive Outreach Email**

> Assunto: Urgente: [Assunto] - Plano de Ação
>
> Caro(a) [Executive Sponsor Name],
>
> Fui informado(a) sobre [describe situation] e quero garantir que estamos tratando disso com a máxima prioridade.
>
> Entendo o impacto que isso está causando no seu negócio e assumo responsabilidade pessoal pela resolução.
>
> **Plano de Ação Imediato**:
> 1. [Action 1] - Responsável: [Owner] - Prazo: [Deadline]
> 2. [Action 2] - Responsável: [Owner] - Prazo: [Deadline]
> 3. [Action 3] - Responsável: [Owner] - Prazo: [Deadline]
>
> Vou fazer check-in com você diariamente até que isso esteja resolvido.
>
> Meu contato direto: [Phone/WhatsApp]
>
> Atenciosamente,
> [CS Director or CEO Name]

**Level 2 Crisis: CS Director Outreach**

> Assunto: Sua experiência com a ConectaPay - Vamos conversar
>
> Olá [Executive Sponsor Name],
>
> Notei que [describe situation] e quero garantir que estamos oferecendo o nível de serviço que você espera.
>
> Gostaria de agendar uma ligação de 30 minutos para:
> - Entender melhor a situação
> - Ouvir suas preocupações diretamente
> - Desenvolver um plano de ação juntos
>
> Meus horários disponíveis:
> - [Date 1]: [Time slots]
> - [Date 2]: [Time slots]
>
> Qual funciona melhor para você?
>
> Abraço,
> [CS Director Name]

### 8.4 Post-Crisis Review

**Within 1 week of resolution**:
- Conduct post-crisis review (internal team)
- Document root cause and resolution
- Identify process improvements
- Update playbooks and prevent recurrence

**Crisis Review Questions**:
1. What happened and why?
2. How did we detect it? Could we have detected it earlier?
3. Was our response effective? What worked well?
4. What could we have done better?
5. What changes do we need to make to prevent recurrence?
6. How is the customer relationship now? What else do we need to do?

## 9. High-Touch CS Operating Model

### 9.1 Team Structure

**Phase 1: Foundation** (0-50 high-touch customers)
- **Head of High-Touch CS** (1)
- **Senior CSMs** (2)
  - Each manages 20-25 enterprise customers
  - Backfill support for high-value SMBs

**Phase 2: Scale** (50-150 high-touch customers)
- **Head of High-Touch CS** (1)
- **Team Lead - Enterprise** (1)
- **Senior CSMs - Enterprise** (3-4)
  - 1:25 ratio for enterprise
- **CSMs - High-Value SMB** (2-3)
  - 1:50 ratio for high-value SMBs

**Phase 3: Maturity** (150+ high-touch customers)
- **VP of Customer Success** (1)
- **Director of High-Touch CS** (1)
- **Team Leads** (2-3)
- **Senior CSMs** (8-10)
- **CSMs** (5-8)
- **Success Operations** (1-2)

### 9.2 Role Definitions

**Senior CSM - Enterprise**:
- **Mission**: Strategic partner for enterprise customers
- **Portfolio**: 20-25 enterprise customers (R$ 5k+ ARR)
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Own customer outcomes and retention
  - Conduct quarterly EBRs
  - Co-create and execute success plans
  - Identify and close expansion opportunities
  - Manage crisis situations
  - Gather strategic feedback for product
- **Skills Required**:
  - 7+ years customer success or account management
  - 3+ years enterprise experience
  - Executive presence (C-level engagement)
  - Strategic thinking and business acumen
  - Fluent Portuguese (native), English (B2+)
- **Compensation**:
  - Base: R$ 12,000-15,000/month
  - Bonus: 25% of salary (portfolio metrics)
  - Commission: 7% of expansion revenue

**CSM - High-Value SMB**:
- **Mission**: Drive growth and success for high-value SMBs
- **Portfolio**: 40-50 high-value SMB customers (R$ 1k-5k ARR)
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Bi-weekly touchpoints and success plan management
  - Monthly success plan reviews
  - Semi-annual business reviews
  - Expansion opportunity identification
  - Customer advocacy development
- **Skills Required**:
  - 3+ years customer success experience
  - SMB business understanding
  - Strong communication and relationship building
  - Data-driven and metrics-focused
  - Fluent Portuguese (native), English (B1+)
- **Compensation**:
  - Base: R$ 8,000-10,000/month
  - Bonus: 20% of salary (portfolio metrics)
  - Commission: 5% of expansion revenue

### 9.3 Operating Rhythms

**Daily Rhythms**:
- **CSM Daily** (30 min): Health review, prioritize outreach, plan touchpoints
- **Team Stand-up** (15 min): Share wins, blockers, priorities

**Weekly Rhythms**:
- **CSM Weekly** (2 hours): Portfolio review, account planning, expansion pipeline
- **Team Meeting** (60 min): Metrics review, best practices, training
- **1:1s with Manager** (30 min per CSM): Coaching, development, support

**Monthly Rhythms**:
- **Team Monthly** (Half-day): Metrics review, skill training, account deep-dives
- **Pipeline Review** (CSM + CS Director): Expansion pipeline, forecasting

**Quarterly Rhythms**:
- **Quarterly Planning** (Full day): OKRs, strategy, roadmap alignment
- **EBR Execution**: Conduct all quarterly EBRs
- **Team Calibration**: Performance reviews, compensation, promotions
