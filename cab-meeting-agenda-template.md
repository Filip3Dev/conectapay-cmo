## Meeting Agenda (90 Minutes)

### 0:00-0:10 (10 min): Welcome & Business Update
**Owner**: CEO or CMO
**Goal**: Set context, build excitement, share progress

**Agenda**:
- Welcome and thank attendees (2 min)
- Quick introductions (if new members) (3 min)
- Business highlights since last meeting (3 min):
  - New customer wins
  - Revenue milestones
  - Product launches
  - Team updates
- Goals for this meeting (2 min)

**Materials**:
- Slide deck (3-5 slides)
- Metrics dashboard (1 slide)

**Preparation**:
- Prepare business update highlights
- Practice transitions
- Prepare for questions

---

### 0:10-0:30 (20 min): Product Roadmap Review
**Owner**: Product Manager
**Goal**: Share upcoming features, gather strategic feedback

**Agenda**:
- Roadmap recap (last quarter's commitments) (3 min)
  - What we delivered
  - What we didn't deliver (and why)
- Upcoming roadmap (next 3-6 months) (10 min)
  - Key themes and priorities
  - Feature walkthrough (with mockups)
  - Timeline and sequencing
- Q&A (7 min)

**Materials**:
- Roadmap slide deck (10-15 slides)
- Mockups or prototypes (Miro/FigJam)
- Feature prioritization framework

**Preparation**:
- Prepare roadmap slides
- Create mockups for key features
- Anticipate questions and objections

**Interactive Element**:
- Live poll: "Which feature excites you most?"
- Miro board: Dot voting on features

---

### 0:30-1:00 (30 min): Feature Feedback & Co-Creation
**Owner**: Product Manager + Customer Success
**Goal**: Deep dive on specific features, collaborative ideation

**Agenda**:
- Feature deep-dive #1 (10 min):
  - Problem statement
  - Proposed solution (mockups)
  - Feedback and discussion
- Feature deep-dive #2 (10 min):
  - Problem statement
  - Proposed solution (mockups)
  - Feedback and discussion
- Open ideation (10 min):
  - "What's missing?"
  - "What would make this irresistible?"
  - Brainstorm and prioritize

**Materials**:
- Feature mockups (Miro/FigJam)
- Feedback forms (digital or physical)
- Sticky notes (virtual or physical)

**Preparation**:
- Prepare 2-3 features for deep-dive
- Create collaborative Miro board
- Prepare open-ended questions

**Interactive Elements**:
- Breakout rooms (small group discussion)
- Live annotation on mockups
- "Rose, Bud, Thorn" exercise

---

### 1:00-1:15 (15 min): Market Trends & Competitive Insights
**Owner**: CEO or CMO
**Goal**: Gather market intelligence, learn from customers

**Agenda**:
- Market trends discussion (5 min):
  - "What are you seeing in the market?"
  - "What's changing in your industry?"
  - "What are your competitors doing?"
- Competitive intelligence (5 min):
  - "What tools are you evaluating?"
  - "What features are competitors launching?"
  - "What would make you switch?"
- Open discussion (5 min)

**Materials**:
- Competitive landscape map (1 slide)
- Trends framework (PESTLE)

**Preparation**:
- Research competitive landscape
- Prepare trend-spotting questions
- Create safe space for honest feedback

**Note**: This section requires trust and psychological safety. Encourage honesty.

---

### 1:15-1:25 (10 min): Success Story Spotlight
**Owner**: CAB Member (rotating)
**Goal**: Celebrate wins, share best practices, inspire others

**Agenda**:
- Member introduction (1 min)
- Their ConectaPay story (5 min):
  - Challenge before ConectaPay
  - Implementation journey
  - Results and ROI
  - Tips and best practices
- Q&A (4 min)

**Materials**:
- Slide deck (optional, 3-5 slides)
- Metrics dashboard (1 slide)

**Preparation**:
- Coordinate with spotlight member 1 week in advance
- Help them prepare slides (if needed)
- Practice their presentation

**Note**: This builds community and advocacy. Record and share (with permission).

---

### 1:25-1:30 (5 min): Action Items & Next Steps
**Owner**: CAB Program Manager
**Goal**: Clear next steps, accountability, closed loop

**Agenda**:
- Recap key insights (2 min):
  - Top 3 feedback themes
  - Top 3 feature requests
  - Top 3 action items
- Assign owners and due dates (2 min):
  - Action item → Owner → Due date
- Next meeting preview (1 min):
  - Date and time
  - Topics to prepare
- Thank you and closing (<1 min)

**Materials**:
- Action items tracker (Google Sheet)
- Next meeting invitation

**Preparation**:
- Capture all action items during meeting
- Assign owners in real-time
- Send meeting notes within 24 hours

## Post-Meeting Checklist

**Within 24 Hours**:
- [ ] Send meeting notes and action items to all attendees
- [ ] Share meeting recording (if approved)
- [ ] Post highlights to CAB Slack/Discord channel
- [ ] Thank participants via email

**Within 48 Hours**:
- [ ] Update product backlog with CAB feedback
- [ ] Assign action items to owners
- [ ] Share CAB insights with broader company (Slack, all-hands)
- [ ] Update CAB portal with roadmap changes

**Within 1 Week**:
- [ ] Follow up on promised items (e.g., "send me that case study")
- [ ] Schedule 1:1 calls for members who wanted more discussion
- [ ] Start work on high-priority action items
- [ ] Monitor CAB engagement (response to notes, questions)

**Before Next Meeting**:
- [ ] Progress update on action items from last meeting
- [ ] Validate that commitments were met
- [ ] Prepare agenda for next meeting
- [ ] Collect new feedback and requests

## Meeting Roles and Responsibilities

**Facilitator (CEO or CMO)**:
- Keep meeting on time (90 minutes max)
- Ensure all voices are heard
- Manage dominant personalities
- Create psychological safety
- Drive to actionable outcomes

**Note Taker (CAB Program Manager)**:
- Capture key insights and quotes
- Track action items with owners
- Record decisions made
- Identify themes and patterns
- Distribute notes within 24 hours

**Product Manager**:
- Present roadmap and features
- Gather feedback and requirements
- Answer product questions
- Follow up on feature requests
- Close the loop on delivered features

**Customer Success Lead**:
- Monitor member engagement
- Identify at-risk members
- Coordinate logistics and materials
- Follow up on action items
- Maintain CAB portal

---

## Meeting Facilitation Tips

**Time Management**:
- Use a visible timer
- Have a timekeeper
- Park off-topic discussions (parking lot)
- Cut tangents gently but firmly

**Engagement**:
- Call on quiet members by name
- Use breakout rooms for small-group discussion
- Use polls and live annotation
- Rotate "success story spotlight" members

**Psychological Safety**:
- Encourage dissenting opinions
- Thank people for tough feedback
- Don't get defensive
- Validate concerns even if you disagree

**Action-Oriented**:
- End every discussion with "so what?"
- Assign owners and due dates in real-time
- Track action items publicly
- Follow up on commitments
