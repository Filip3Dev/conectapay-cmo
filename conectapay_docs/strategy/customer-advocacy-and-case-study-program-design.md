## Executive Summary

### Program Vision

Transform ConectaPay customers into our most powerful growth engine through a strategic advocacy program that identifies, nurtures, and leverages customer champions at every stage of their journey—driving acquisition through social proof, retention through deeper engagement, and expansion through authentic advocacy.

### Strategic Objectives

| Objective | 12-Month Target | 24-Month Target | Business Impact |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Advocate Pipeline** | 100 identified, 50 active | 300 identified, 150 active | Sustainable growth engine |
| **Case Study Production** | 4-6/month (48-72/year) | 8-10/month (96-120/year) | Primary sales collateral |
| **Referenceable Customers** | 20% of customer base | 35% of customer base | 2x sales velocity |
| **Advocate-Influenced Revenue** | 30% of new ARR | 50% of new ARR | R$ 900k/year savings |
| **Customer Advocacy Score (CAS)** | 60/100 | 75/100 | Leading indicator of NRR |
| **Advocate Retention Rate** | 85% | 90% | Higher NRR (120%+) |

### Program Philosophy

**Authenticity Over Transactions**
> We don't "incentivize" advocacy—we recognize and amplify authentic enthusiasm. The best advocates aren't motivated by rewards; they're motivated by the desire to help others succeed, share their own success, and be part of something bigger.

**Relationship Over Recruitment**
> Advocacy is a byproduct of exceptional customer experiences, not a program you can manufacture. We invest deeply in customer success first, then invite our happiest customers to share their stories—never the other way around.

**Quality Over Quantity**
> One authentic, well-documented case study from a genuine customer success story is worth 100 superficial testimonials. We focus on depth of impact over breadth of content.

**Mutual Value**
> Every advocacy interaction must create value for both parties. Customers gain visibility, networking, and influence; ConectaPay gains authentic social proof and market insights.

## Part 2: Case Study Program Design

### 2.1 Case Study Strategy

**Strategic Role of Case Studies**
Case studies are ConectaPay's primary social proof asset. They serve multiple functions across the customer lifecycle:

| Business Function | Case Study Role | Key Metrics |
|-------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Marketing** | Top-of-funnel content, SEO, thought leadership | Views, shares, leads generated |
| **Sales** | Mid-to-late funnel proof, objection handling | Deals influenced, close rate lift |
| **Customer Success** | Onboarding inspiration, best practices | Feature adoption, time-to-value |
| **Product** | User research, feedback collection | Features influenced, roadmap alignment |
| **Brand** | Market positioning, competitive differentiation | Share of voice, sentiment |

### 2.2 Case Study Typology

**Type 1: Problem-Solution Case Studies**
- **Focus**: Customer challenge → ConectaPay solution → Measurable outcome
- **Format**: 1,500-2,500 words, data-driven, quote-heavy
- **Use Case**: Mid-funnel consideration, sales objections
- **Production Effort**: High (4-6 hours total)
- **Business Impact**: High (direct sales influence)

**Type 2: Implementation Journey Case Studies**
- **Focus**: Onboarding process → Early challenges → Quick wins → Long-term success
- **Format**: 1,000-1,500 words, timeline-based, tactical
- **Use Case**: Customer onboarding, implementation guidance
- **Production Effort**: Medium (3-4 hours total)
- **Business Impact**: Medium (retention and expansion)

**Type 3: Segment Spotlight Case Studies**
- **Focus**: Industry-specific challenges, segment trends, peer insights
- **Format**: 800-1,200 words, segment-focused, comparative
- **Use Case**: Segment marketing, ABM campaigns
- **Production Effort**: Medium (3-4 hours total)
- **Business Impact**: Medium-High (segment targeting)

**Type 4: Feature Deep-Dive Case Studies**
- **Focus**: Specific feature use case, creative application, results
- **Format**: 600-1,000 words, feature-focused, educational
- **Use Case**: Product marketing, feature adoption
- **Production Effort**: Low-Medium (2-3 hours total)
- **Business Impact**: Medium (feature adoption)

**Type 5: Video Case Studies**
- **Focus**: Customer story in their voice, emotional connection
- **Format**: 3-5 minute video, documentary-style
- **Use Case**: Social media, events, website hero
- **Production Effort**: High (1 day filming + editing)
- **Business Impact**: High (brand awareness, trust)

#### Phase 3: Interview Execution (Week 2-3)

**Interview Preparation**

**Pre-Interview Research (30 min)**
- Review customer profile and usage data
- Identify specific metrics and milestones
- Research customer's industry and competitors
- Review previous feedback and support tickets
- Prepare personalized questions

**Interview Logistics**

```
Format: Video call (Zoom/Google Meet)
Duration: 30-60 minutes
Recording: Yes (with permission)
Participants: Customer + Interviewer (1-2 people)
Scheduling: Calendly link, customer's timezone
```

**Interview Question Bank**

**Section 1: Background & Context (5-10 min)**
1. Tell us about your business—what do you do, who do you serve?
2. How did you get started in [industry]?
3. What was your biggest challenge before ConectaPay?
4. How were you handling [specific problem] before?

**Section 2: Discovery & Decision (5-10 min)**
5. How did you first hear about ConectaPay?
6. What made you decide to try it?
7. What were your biggest concerns or hesitations?
8. What was the deciding factor?

**Section 3: Implementation (5-10 min)**
9. What was the onboarding experience like?
10. How long did it take to get up and running?
11. What was your first automation or campaign?
12. Were there any challenges? How did you overcome them?

**Section 4: Results & Impact (10-15 min)**
13. What specific results have you achieved?
14. Can you share any numbers (revenue, time saved, etc.)?
15. What surprised you most about using ConectaPay?
16. What's the one thing you couldn't live without now?

**Section 5: Advice & Reflection (5-10 min)**
17. What would you say to someone considering ConectaPay?
18. What's one thing you wish you knew when you started?
19. How has ConectaPay changed your business?
20. What's next for you and your business?

**Probing Techniques**
- **Dig for specifics**: "Can you give me an example of that?"
- **Quantify outcomes**: "How much time/money did that save?"
- **Capture emotions**: "How did that make you feel?"
- **Before/after contrast**: "What was life like before vs. after?"

**Writing Guidelines**

**Voice and Tone**
- Write in the customer's voice (use their words)
- Be authentic, not promotional
- Focus on the customer's journey, not ConectaPay features
- Use active voice, simple language
- Portuguese: Natural, conversational, professional

**Quality Standards**
- Every claim must be backed by data or a quote
- No hyperbole or exaggeration
- Balanced: mention challenges, not just successes
- Specific: use real numbers, dates, details
- Credible: link to customer website/social when possible

**SEO Optimization**
- Include target keywords: "automação WhatsApp", "recuperação de vendas", "[segmento]"
- Optimize headline: [Customer Name] achieves [Result] with ConectaPay
- Meta description: 150-char summary
- Alt text for images
- Internal linking to related content

**Step 1: Internal Review (2 days)**

**Revision Guidelines**
- **Grammar/Clarity**: Edit without asking

## Part 4: Recognition and Rewards Design

### 4.1 Recognition Philosophy

**Core Principle**: The most effective advocates are motivated by intrinsic rewards—recognition, relationship, and impact—not extrinsic financial incentives.

**Recognition Hierarchy of Needs**

```
1. RECOGNITION (Being Seen)
   - Public acknowledgment
   - Visibility in community
   - Appreciation for contributions

2. RELATIONSHIP (Being Connected)
   - Access to ConectaPay team
   - Networking with peers
   - Insider status

3. IMPACT (Being Valuable)
   - Influence on product
   - Helping others succeed
   - Shaping the market

4. REWARDS (Being Compensated) - Secondary
   - Financial incentives
   - Product credits
   - Gifts and perks
```

### 5.2 Activity-Level ROI Calculation

**Reference Call ROI**

```
Cost Components:
- Advocate gift: R$ 200
- Advocacy Manager time: 1 hr @ R$ 150/hr = R$ 150
- Sales time: 0.5 hr @ R$ 200/hr = R$ 100
Total Cost per Call: R$ 450

Revenue Impact:
- Incremental close rate: 20% (baseline 20% → 40% with reference)
- Average deal size: R$ 3,000/year
- Value per call: R$ 3,000 × 20% = R$ 600

ROI = (R$ 600 - R$ 450) / R$ 450 = 33%

Break-even: 1 closed deal per 1.3 calls

Leverage Point: Focus on high-value deals (R$ 5k+ ARR) for 3x ROI
```

**Case Study ROI**

```
Cost Components:
- Interview: 1 hr @ R$ 150/hr = R$ 150
- Writing: 4 hrs @ R$ 150/hr = R$ 600
- Design: 3 hrs @ R$ 100/hr = R$ 300
- Customer gift: R$ 500
Total Cost: R$ 1,550

Revenue Impact (Year 1):
- Views: 500/month × 12 = 6,000 views
- Conversion: 2% → 120 leads
- Close rate: 10% → 12 customers
- Revenue: 12 × R$ 3,000 = R$ 36,000

ROI = (R$ 36,000 - R$ 1,550) / R$ 1,550 = 2,222%

Payback: < 1 month

Lifetime Value (3 years):
- 36 customers × R$ 3,000 × 3 years = R$ 324,000
- Cost amortized: R$ 1,550 × 10 (repubs) = R$ 15,500
- Lifetime ROI: 2,000%+
```

**Webinar ROI**

```
Cost Components:
- Advocate gift: R$ 500
- Marketing time: 4 hrs @ R$ 150/hr = R$ 600
- Platform cost: R$ 100
Total Cost: R$ 1,200

Revenue Impact:
- Registrants: 100
- Attendees: 35 (35% show rate)
- Qualified leads: 15 (40% of attendees)
- Closed deals: 3 (20% close rate)
- Revenue: 3 × R$ 3,000 = R$ 9,000

ROI = (R$ 9,000 - R$ 1,200) / R$ 1,200 = 650%

Payback: < 2 months

Additional Benefits:
- Content library value (recording)
- Brand awareness (100 registrants)
- Partner relationship (advocate)
```

### 5.3 Advocate Health Scoring

**Advocate Health Score (AHS)**

```
AHS = (Activity Score × 0.4) + (Satisfaction Score × 0.3) +
      (Engagement Score × 0.2) + (Tenure Score × 0.1)

Where:
Activity Score (0-100):
- 0 activities in 90 days: 0
- 1 activity in 90 days: 40
- 2 activities in 90 days: 70
- 3+ activities in 90 days: 100

Satisfaction Score (0-100):
- Average of post-activity ratings (1-5 scale converted to 0-100)
- NPS response (if available)

Engagement Score (0-100):
- Email open rate: 40 points
- Community participation: 30 points
- Event attendance: 30 points

Tenure Score (0-100):
- 0-6 months: 50
- 6-12 months: 75
- 12+ months: 100

Health Segments:
- 80-100: Healthy (green) - Maintain engagement
- 60-79: At-risk (yellow) - Re-engagement needed
- 0-59: Churned (red) - Win-back or archive
```

## Appendix B: Program Glossary

**Advocate**: Any customer who actively participates in advocacy activities (testimonials, references, case studies, etc.)

**Champion**: High-performing advocate (Tier 2) who regularly participates and has demonstrated success with ConectaPay

**Advisory Board**: Top-tier advocates (Tier 1) who provide strategic guidance on product and business direction

**Advocate Candidate**: Customer identified as potential advocate based on signals (NPS, usage, engagement)

**Advocate Pipeline**: System to track and manage advocates through identification, recruitment, and activation

**Reference Call**: Call between advocate and prospect to share experience and answer questions

**Case Study**: Detailed success story documenting customer journey, implementation, and ROI

**Testimonial**: Short quote or statement about customer experience

**Advocate Activity**: Any action where an advocate represents ConectaPay (reference call, case study, webinar, etc.)

**Advocate Tier**: Level of advocate engagement and recognition (Tier 3/2/1)

**NPS**: Net Promoter Score - customer loyalty metric (0-10)

**CAS**: Customer Advocacy Score - composite measure of advocacy health (0-100)

**AHS**: Advocate Health Score - individual advocate engagement metric (0-100)

**ROI**: Return on Investment - revenue generated vs. cost invested

**CAC**: Customer Acquisition Cost - average cost to acquire a new customer

**NRR**: Net Revenue Retention - revenue retained from existing customers (including expansion)

**LTV**: Lifetime Value - total revenue from a customer over their relationship
