## Executive Summary

### Program Purpose

Transform enthusiastic customers into ConectaPay's most powerful marketing asset through a structured advocacy program that identifies, nurtures, and leverages customer champions at every stage of their journey.

### Launch Objectives (90 Days)

| Metric | Day 30 | Day 60 | Day 90 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| **Advocates Identified** | 20 | 50 | 100 |
| **Advocates Recruited** | 10 | 30 | 50 |
| **Referenceable Customers** | 5 | 12 | 20 |
| **Advocate Activities Completed** | 15 | 40 | 75 |
| **Sales Calls with Advocates** | 3 | 10 | 25 |
| **Content Generated** | 3 | 8 | 15 |

### 12-Month Targets

- **100+ advocates** identified and nurtured across all segments
- **30+ active champions** participating regularly in advocacy activities
- **12 advisory board members** providing strategic guidance
- **50+ advocate activities** (references, case studies, speaking, testimonials)
- **30% reduction** in sales cycle when advocate involved
- **2x higher close rate** on deals with advocate participation

## Part 2: Identification & Recruitment System

### 2.1 Automated Identification Signals

#### Tier 3: Advocate Candidates (Top 20%)

**Data Sources & Triggers:**

```
NPS Survey Response = 9 or 10
  → Auto-tag as "Promoter"
  → Trigger: Advocacy outreach email (Day 2)

Usage Metrics (Monthly):
  - Automation runs: Top quartile by segment
  - Messages sent: >500/month
  - Revenue recovered: >R$ 2.000/month
  → Auto-score: +10 points

Engagement Metrics:
  - Email open rate: >50%
  - Feature adoption: >70% of activated features
  - Support sentiment: Positive tone in 3+ tickets
  → Auto-score: +10 points

Social Signals:
  - LinkedIn mention: Tagged @conectapay
  - Instagram post: Tagged @conectapay
  → Manual review: Potential fast-track

Referral Activity:
  - Referred 1+ customer (even without program)
  → Auto-score: +15 points
```

**Scoring Threshold (0-100 points):**
- **70+ points**: Advocate candidate → Auto-add to pipeline
- **50-69 points**: Monitor → Re-score in 30 days
- **<50 points**: Not ready → Continue nurturing

#### Tier 2: Champions (Top 5%)

**Additional Requirements:**
- Tenure: 6+ months as customer
- ROI: Quantifiable success (3x+ ROI documented)
- Proactive: Reached out with feedback/ideas 2+ times
- Visible: Active LinkedIn presence (500+ connections)
- Speaker: Completed 1+ recorded call or video

**Screening Process:**
1. **Automated flag** when all Tier 3 signals + tenure met
2. **CSM verification** call (15 min) to confirm willingness
3. **Case study interview** to document success story
4. **Champion invitation** extended after case study approved

#### Tier 1: Advisory Board (Top 1%)

**Additional Requirements:**
- Strategic mindset: Product vision feedback quality
- Industry authority: Recognized in segment (speaking, press, awards)
- Leadership: Owns business with 10+ employees or manages multi-location
- Long-term commitment: 12+ month outlook expressed
- Network: Connections to 5+ potential customers

**Vetting Process:**
1. **Founder/VP CS outreach** for exploratory call (30 min)
2. **Product alignment** discussion with product team
3. **Values assessment** through behavioral questions
4. **Conflict check** (no competitor relationships)
5. **Advisory board invitation** with term sheet

## Part 7: Risk Mitigation & Troubleshooting

### 7.1 Common Challenges & Solutions

#### Challenge 1: Low Advocate Participation

**Symptoms:**
- Advocates not responding to requests
- Low engagement in community
- Few advocates completing activities

**Root Causes:**
- Over-asking (too many requests)
- Poor matching (wrong activities for wrong advocates)
- Lack of recognition (advocates feel undervalued)
- Unclear expectations (advocates don't know what's expected)

**Solutions:**
1. **Implement "ask frequency" limits** - Maximum 1 request per month per advocate
2. **Improve matching** - Better scoring algorithm for activity assignment
3. **Increase recognition** - More public appreciation, spotlight features
4. **Clarify expectations** - Clear onboarding about time commitment

**Prevention:**
- Survey advocates about preferred activities and frequency
- Track advocate burnout signals (declining requests, late responses)
- Regular check-ins to assess satisfaction

---

#### Challenge 2: Negative Advocate Experiences

**Symptoms:**
- Advocates complain about poor experiences
- Advocates stop participating
- Negative word-of-mouth from advocates

**Root Causes:**
- Poor preparation (advocate not briefed properly)
- Unreasonable expectations (asked for too much)
- Poor sales experiences (advocates feel "used")
- Lack of follow-up (no thank you or recognition)

**Solutions:**
1. **Improve preparation** - Always send advocate briefing sheet 24h before
2. **Set clear expectations** - Be specific about time commitment and what's needed
3. **Attend sales calls** - Advocacy Manager or CSM should attend first few calls
4. **Mandatory follow-up** - Always send thank you within 24 hours

**Prevention:**
- Role-play reference calls with sales team
- Provide advocates with exit strategy (can decline without penalty)
- Regular feedback surveys after each activity

---

#### Challenge 3: Sales Team Over-Reliance

**Symptoms:**
- Sales team requests references for every deal
- Advocates overwhelmed by requests
- Low fulfillment rates (can't keep up)

**Root Causes:**
- No clear criteria for when to request references
- Sales team sees references as magic bullet
- No limits on request volume

**Solutions:**
1. **Clear criteria** - References only for deals > R$ 3,000 ARR or at negotiation stage
2. **Request limits** - Maximum 10 reference requests per week
3. **Prioritization** - Tier 1 prospects get priority for advocate matching
4. **Diversification** - Use other social proof (case studies, testimonials) for smaller deals

**Prevention:**
- Train sales team on when references are appropriate
- Provide sales team with alternative social proof tools
- Monitor request volume and push back if excessive

---

#### Challenge 4: Quality Issues (Advocate Performance)

**Symptoms:**
- Advocates give poor references (negative, unprepared)
- Case studies lack detail or impact
- Webinars poorly attended or received

**Root Causes:**
- Wrong advocates selected (not actually happy or articulate)
- Poor preparation (advocates not briefed)
- Lack of training (advocates don't know what to expect)

**Solutions:**
1. **Better vetting** - Only recruit advocates who have demonstrated enthusiasm
2. **Mandatory preparation** - All activities require briefing and prep call
3. **Practice sessions** - Role-play reference calls before real ones
4. **Feedback loops** - Provide constructive feedback to advocates

**Prevention:**
- Start with low-risk activities (testimonials) before high-risk (reference calls)
- Gradually increase advocate responsibility based on performance
- Have backup advocates ready if primary advocate underperforms

---

#### Challenge 5: Legal/Compliance Issues

**Symptoms:**
- Advocates make unauthorized claims about product
- Testimonials violate advertising regulations
- Data privacy concerns (LGPD)

**Root Causes:**
- No clear guidelines for advocates
- No review process for advocate content
- Lack of proper consent and permissions

**Solutions:**
1. **Create advocate guidelines** - Clear dos and don'ts for public statements
2. **Mandatory review** - All advocate content reviewed before publication
3. **Proper consent** - Testimonial usage consent forms, case study release agreements
4. **Legal review** - Have legal review all program materials and templates

**Prevention:**
- Train advocates on what they can and cannot say
- Provide advocates with talking points (but allow authenticity)
- Regular legal audits of program compliance

### Appendix B: Program Glossary

**Advocate**: Any customer who actively participates in advocacy activities

**Champion**: High-performing advocate (Tier 2) who regularly participates and has demonstrated success

**Advisory Board**: Top-tier advocates (Tier 1) who provide strategic guidance

**Advocate Candidate**: Customer identified as potential advocate based on signals

**Advocate Pipeline**: System to track and manage advocates through identification, recruitment, and activation

**Reference Call**: Call between advocate and prospect to share experience

**Case Study**: Detailed success story documenting ROI and results

**Testimonial**: Short quote or statement about customer experience

**Advocate Activity**: Any action where an advocate represents ConectaPay (reference call, case study, webinar, etc)

**Advocate Tier**: Level of advocate engagement and recognition (Tier 3/2/1)

**NPS**: Net Promoter Score - customer loyalty metric (0-10)

**K-Factor**: Viral coefficient - average number of new customers each customer brings

**CAC**: Customer Acquisition Cost - average cost to acquire a new customer

**ROI**: Return on Investment - revenue generated vs. cost invested
